## The Intent

The interviewer is assessing strategic test design capability at scale. Senior testers are expected to own the testing strategy for a whole product — not just a feature or sprint. They want to see structured decomposition, persona-aware coverage, risk prioritisation, and a practical execution model that works within real team capacity.

---

## The STAR Script

**Situation:** I was appointed lead QA engineer for a B2B SaaS platform serving three distinct user personas: system administrators (who configured the platform and managed users), end users (who performed day-to-day work within the platform), and auditors (who had read-only access to compliance reports and audit trails). The product had 14 functional modules, a complex permission system, and a six-week release cycle.

**Task:** I needed to design a manual testing strategy that gave each persona's workflows appropriate coverage, prioritised by risk, and was executable within a team of three QA engineers across the six-week cycle.

**Action:** I structured the strategy across four dimensions. First, **product decomposition**: I mapped all 14 modules against the three personas — which modules each persona accessed, which were persona-exclusive, and which were shared. This produced a coverage matrix that immediately revealed that 6 modules were admin-only, 5 were end-user-only, and 3 were accessed by all three. The shared modules — authentication, notifications, and reporting — were the highest complexity and risk, as a defect there would affect all user types simultaneously. Second, **risk-based prioritisation**: I scored each module on two axes — likelihood of defect (based on code churn, complexity, and recent change history) and business impact per persona (revenue impact for end users, compliance risk for auditors, operational risk for admins). This gave me a risk tier for each module-persona combination, which determined testing depth and frequency. Third, **persona-aware test design**: for each module I designed test cases from the perspective of each relevant persona — not just "does the feature work" but "does it work in the context of what this persona is trying to achieve?" An admin configuring a permission rule and an end user experiencing that permission rule are two completely different test scenarios with different risk profiles. Fourth, **capacity allocation**: I mapped the risk tiers against team capacity — senior QA covered Tier 1 (highest risk, cross-persona), mid-level QA covered Tier 2 (significant risk, single persona), and Tier 3 (low risk, stable) was covered through lightweight exploratory sessions rather than scripted test execution.

**Result:** The strategy produced a 180-test-case scripted suite with clear persona tagging and risk tier classification, plus a library of 12 exploratory charters for Tier 3 coverage. In the first release cycle under the strategy, two critical defects were caught in the shared authentication module — both had cross-persona impact that would have been missed if testing had been conducted without persona awareness. The capacity allocation model meant the team completed the full strategy within the six-week cycle with no overtime.

## Key Terms

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **User Persona** | A distinct type of user with specific goals, permissions, and workflows — each requiring tailored test coverage. |
| **Product Decomposition** | Breaking a complex product into testable domains or modules for structured coverage planning. |
| **Persona-Aware Testing** | Designing test cases from the perspective of a specific user type — testing not just feature functionality but user-goal achievement. |
| **Coverage Matrix** | A document mapping modules to personas and risk tiers — the backbone of a multi-persona test strategy. |
| **Risk Tier** | A classification of test areas by combined likelihood of defect and business impact — drives depth and frequency of testing. |
| **Capacity Allocation** | Matching test effort to team capacity based on risk tier — ensures the highest-value work is prioritised. |
