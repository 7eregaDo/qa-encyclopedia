## The Intent

The interviewer is assessing both depth of exploratory testing knowledge and the ability to operationalise it across a team. Many testers know what exploratory testing is; fewer can articulate how to scale it without it becoming either too ad-hoc (low consistency) or too scripted (loses its advantage). Heuristics are the bridge — and knowing them signals genuine expertise.

---

## The STAR Script

**Situation:** I was leading a QA team of four engineers across a product that was releasing every two weeks. We had a strong scripted regression suite, but exploratory testing was inconsistent — different engineers explored different things, sessions were undocumented, and the team had no shared language for what to look for during exploration.

**Task:** I needed to make exploratory testing consistent, teachable, and documentable across the team — without turning it into scripted testing by another name.

**Action:** I introduced a heuristics-based exploratory framework built around three well-established models. First, **SFDIPOT** (also known as the San Francisco Depot heuristic): Structure, Function, Data, Interfaces, Platform, Operations, Time. I used this as a charter planning tool — before each exploratory session, the tester would choose one or two SFDIPOT dimensions as their focus. A session focused on "Data" would explore what happens with empty data, maximum data, corrupted data, and boundary data. A session focused on "Time" would explore concurrency, session expiry, date boundaries, and slow network conditions. This gave each session a defined angle without scripting the steps. Second, **CRUD testing** for data-centric features: does the feature correctly Create, Read, Update, and Delete records? Do all four operations handle edge cases, permission restrictions, and concurrent access? Third, **FCC CUTS VIDS** for rapid feature assessment: Familiar, Consistent, Correct, Unique, Tested by others, Secure, Valuable, Interesting, Deep, Surprising — a checklist of dimensions to consider when first encountering a new feature. I ran two workshops introducing these heuristics with practical exercises, then introduced a charter template that required testers to state which heuristic dimension they were exploring and what they were looking for. Sessions were summarised in a shared Confluence space using a standard debrief format.

**Result:** Within three sprints, exploratory session quality became significantly more consistent across the team. The shared debrief space meant findings in one session informed charters in the next — exploration became cumulative rather than starting from scratch each time. Defects found through exploratory testing increased by 40% in the following quarter compared to the same period the previous year — not because the team explored more, but because they explored more systematically. A junior engineer told me the heuristics had transformed exploratory testing from "I'm not sure what I'm supposed to be looking for" to a genuinely structured skill they could develop.

## Seniority Shift

| Level | Expected Depth |
|-------|---------------|
| **Entry** | Use a charter to focus an exploratory session and document findings in session notes. |
| **Mid** | Apply one or two heuristics deliberately in an exploratory session and explain the rationale for the chosen focus. |
| **Senior** | Build a team-wide heuristics framework — selecting, teaching, and operationalising multiple heuristics, with a charter library and debrief documentation standard that makes exploratory testing consistent and cumulative. |

---

## Key Terms

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Heuristic** | A mental model or rule of thumb that guides a tester toward productive test ideas — not a checklist but a thinking tool. |
| **SFDIPOT** | Structure, Function, Data, Interfaces, Platform, Operations, Time — a comprehensive heuristic for exploratory charter planning. |
| **CRUD Testing** | Testing Create, Read, Update, and Delete operations for any data-centric feature. |
| **Charter Library** | A collection of reusable exploratory charters organised by heuristic dimension — builds cumulative team knowledge. |
| **Session Debrief** | A post-session summary of what was tested, what was found, what questions arose, and what to explore next. |
| **Cumulative Exploration** | An approach where findings from previous sessions inform the focus of future ones — making exploration progressively smarter. |
