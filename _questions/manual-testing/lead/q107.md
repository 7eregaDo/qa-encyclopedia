## The Intent

The interviewer is assessing people leadership maturity and the ability to think about talent strategy in a changing organisational context. Lead QA professionals must be able to articulate a development vision for their team that is honest about the changing landscape, respectful of diverse career goals, and practically structured — not a vague commitment to "growth opportunities."

---

## The STAR Script

**Situation:** I led a QA team of nine engineers, six of whom were primarily manual testers. The company was investing heavily in automation and there was an unspoken expectation in the engineering culture that "good QA engineers become automation engineers." Three of my six manual testers had told me in 1:1s that they felt their career path was unclear, and one had said directly: "I don't want to be an automation engineer — does that mean I have no future here?"

**Task:** I needed to build a career development framework that was honest, differentiated, and gave manual testers a credible path forward — whether they wanted to move toward automation, deepen their manual expertise, or transition into adjacent roles.

**Action:** I designed the framework around three career tracks, all treated as equally valid. First, **the Quality Engineering track**: for testers who wanted to develop automation skills, I defined a structured path — from manual-only through hybrid (manual + scripted API testing) to full quality engineering (automation-first). I provided learning resources, pairing opportunities with automation engineers, and stretch project assignments that built automation skills incrementally without requiring a wholesale identity change. Second, **the Quality Specialist track**: for testers whose strength and passion was in human-judgment-dependent quality work, I defined a deepening path — from competent manual tester through advanced exploratory practitioner to Quality Specialist, a senior role focused on complex investigation, usability evaluation, and quality intelligence. I created explicit role expectations and advancement criteria for each level, making the path as concrete as the automation track. Third, **the Adjacent Role track**: for testers interested in moving into product management, UX research, or business analysis — roles that shared significant skill overlap with experienced manual testers — I created a formal bridge programme: a rotation into the adjacent team for one sprint per quarter, mentoring from a senior person in the target role, and a structured transition plan for those who wanted to make the move. I presented the framework to the full team in a dedicated session, naming each track explicitly and making clear that all three were respected and supported paths.

**Result:** The one engineer who had expressed most concern chose the Quality Specialist track and within 18 months was the team's most advanced exploratory practitioner — running heuristic workshops for the whole QA community and contributing directly to product strategy through quality intelligence work. Two engineers moved into the Quality Engineering track and became strong automation contributors. One engineer transitioned into a product analyst role via the Adjacent Role bridge. Team retention over the following two years was 100% — no attrition from career uncertainty. The framework was adopted by the wider engineering people team as a model for technical career development.

## Seniority Shift

| Level | Expected Depth |
|-------|---------------|
| **Entry** | Describe your own career goals and what skills you are developing to achieve them. |
| **Mid** | Describe supporting a junior engineer's development with a structured plan and regular feedback. |
| **Senior** | Define career progression expectations for a QA team area and support individual engineers in navigating them. |
| **Lead** | Design a multi-track career development framework for a diverse QA team — honouring manual expertise, providing automation pathways, and creating bridges to adjacent roles — with explicit advancement criteria and organisational credibility. |

## Key Terms

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Career Track** | A defined progression pathway within a discipline — multiple valid tracks prevent a single-direction career model. |
| **Quality Specialist** | A senior manual testing role focused on complex investigation, exploratory expertise, and quality intelligence — a credible alternative to the automation engineering track. |
| **Adjacent Role Bridge** | A structured programme enabling transitions from QA into related roles (product, UX, business analysis) — leverages skill overlap. |
| **Explicit Advancement Criteria** | Defined, written expectations for what is required to progress from one level to the next — removes ambiguity and subjectivity from career conversations. |
| **Retention Through Career Clarity** | The principle that people stay in organisations where they can see a credible future — career frameworks are a primary retention tool. |
| **Hybrid Engineer** | A QA engineer who practises both manual and automated testing — a natural intermediate stage in the Quality Engineering track. |
