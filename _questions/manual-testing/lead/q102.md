## The Intent

The interviewer is assessing organisational design thinking and the ability to build capability infrastructure at scale. Lead-level QA professionals in multi-team organisations must be able to create the conditions for consistent, high-quality manual testing across teams they do not directly control. This question distinguishes leaders who build durable capability from those who create short-lived process mandates.

---

## The STAR Script

**Situation:** I was Head of Quality across a product organisation with eight squads, each with one or two embedded QA engineers. Testing practices varied significantly — some squads had mature, well-maintained test suites and structured exploratory practices; others had ad-hoc processes and inconsistent defect reporting. There was no shared knowledge infrastructure, no common standards, and QA engineers felt isolated within their squads with no professional community.

**Task:** I needed to build a centre of excellence that raised the baseline across all squads, created a professional community for QA engineers, and established shared standards — without imposing central control that would reduce squad autonomy or increase overhead.

**Action:** I built the CoE across four pillars. First, **shared standards — the thin layer**: working collaboratively with QA engineers from all eight squads, I defined the minimum shared standards that would deliver organisation-wide value: a common defect report format (so defects were actionable regardless of which squad raised them), a shared definition of done quality checklist (the baseline every story must meet), and a common test case quality standard (specific, assertable expected results, complete preconditions). Critically, these were standards for outcomes, not methods — squads could use any tool, framework, or approach they chose, as long as their outputs met the standard. Second, **knowledge sharing infrastructure**: I established a monthly QA community of practice — a 60-minute session alternating between a skill-building topic (e.g., heuristic-based exploratory testing, accessibility testing techniques) and a case study from one squad's recent testing work. Sessions were recorded and indexed in a shared Confluence space. I also created a "QA Playbook" — a living document of team-contributed practices, charter libraries, testing patterns, and tool guides — owned collectively, not by me. Third, **cross-squad calibration**: once per quarter I ran a "test case calibration session" where QA engineers from different squads reviewed each other's test cases against the quality standard. This built shared understanding of what "good" looked like in practice far more effectively than any document. Fourth, **career visibility**: I established a QA competency framework visible to all engineers and managers, making the path from junior to senior to lead explicit and giving QA engineers a professional identity beyond their squad role.

**Result:** Within six months, defect report quality was consistent across all eight squads — the previously wide variance had narrowed significantly. The community of practice had 100% attendance from QA engineers and was voluntarily attended by several developers and product managers. Cross-squad calibration sessions surfaced two cases where different squads had solved the same testing problem differently — leading to shared solutions adopted across the organisation. Three QA engineers cited the CoE and career framework as the reason they had chosen to stay at the company when approached by recruiters.

## Seniority Shift

| Level | Expected Depth |
|-------|---------------|
| **Entry** | Follow team standards and contribute knowledge to shared documentation. |
| **Mid** | Help establish team-level testing standards and onboard new QA engineers to existing practices. |
| **Senior** | Define testing standards for a team and build a lightweight knowledge-sharing practice within a single squad or product area. |
| **Lead** | Design and build a multi-team CoE — thin shared standards, community of practice, cross-squad calibration, and career infrastructure — that raises organisational capability without centralising control. |

## Key Terms

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Centre of Excellence (CoE)** | An organisational model providing shared standards, expertise, and knowledge infrastructure to multiple teams without centralising control. |
| **Thin Standards Layer** | The minimum set of shared standards that deliver cross-team value — everything else is left to squad discretion. |
| **Community of Practice** | A regular forum where practitioners across teams share knowledge, build skills, and develop professional identity. |
| **QA Playbook** | A collectively owned, living document of team-contributed practices, patterns, and reference material. |
| **Cross-Squad Calibration** | Structured peer review of testing artefacts across teams — builds shared understanding of quality standards in practice. |
| **Competency Framework** | A defined set of skills and behaviours expected at each career level — provides career visibility and professional identity. |
