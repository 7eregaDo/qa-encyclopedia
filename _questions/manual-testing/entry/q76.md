## The Intent

The interviewer is testing a core practical skill. A QA engineer who finds bugs but can't communicate them clearly creates friction rather than value. They want to see that you understand every component of a good bug report and why each matters — not just that you know a template exists.

---

## The STAR Script

**Situation:** Early in my career I raised a defect that was returned to me by the developer marked "cannot reproduce." When I looked at my report, I understood why — I had written "the page breaks when you add an item to the cart," with no environment details, no specific steps, and no screenshot.

**Task:** I needed to learn how to write bug reports that developers could act on immediately, without coming back to me for clarification.

**Action:** I developed a consistent structure for every bug report I wrote from that point: **Title** — a specific, searchable summary (e.g., "Cart total displays £0.00 after applying a promo code on iOS Safari 16"); **Environment** — browser/device, OS version, test environment, user account or role used; **Preconditions** — the exact state the system must be in before the steps begin; **Steps to reproduce** — numbered, atomic steps that anyone could follow without prior knowledge; **Expected result** — what should happen according to the requirements or reasonable expectation; **Actual result** — precisely what happened instead; **Severity and priority** — my assessment with brief rationale; **Attachments** — screenshot or screen recording capturing the defect in context. For intermittent defects I added a reproducibility rate (e.g., "reproduced 4 out of 5 attempts") and any correlation I had noticed (e.g., "only occurs when logged in as an admin role").

**Result:** After adopting this structure, I had zero "cannot reproduce" returns in the following three months. A senior developer told me my bug reports were the clearest on the team. The format was later adopted as the team's standard defect template, reducing back-and-forth across all reporters.

| Level | Expected Depth |

## Key Terms

| Component | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Title** | A concise, specific summary — searchable and self-explanatory without reading the body. |
| **Environment** | Browser, OS, device, app version, and test environment — essential for reproducibility. |
| **Preconditions** | The exact system state before the steps begin. |
| **Steps to Reproduce** | Numbered, atomic instructions anyone can follow to trigger the defect. |
| **Expected Result** | What the system should do according to requirements or convention. |
| **Actual Result** | What the system actually did — described factually, not interpretively. |
| **Severity** | The technical impact of the defect on the system. |
| **Priority** | The business urgency of fixing the defect. |
| **Reproducibility Rate** | How consistently the defect can be triggered — critical for intermittent issues. |
