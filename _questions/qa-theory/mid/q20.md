## The Intent

The interviewer wants to see that you proactively prevent defects rather than reactively find them. Shift-left is a mindset as much as a practice — it demonstrates that you understand the economics of defect detection and can influence the team's workflow to build quality in from the start.

---

## The STAR Script

**Situation:** Our team had a persistent problem: 60–70% of defects were being found in system testing, days before release. Each one triggered a scramble — fixes, re-tests, delays, and stress.

**Task:** I proposed a shift-left initiative to move defect discovery upstream, where fixes are cheaper and less disruptive.

**Action:** I introduced three practices across the SDLC. First, **story kick-offs**: before development started on any story, I joined a 15-minute session with the developer and product owner to walk through the acceptance criteria, ask "what could go wrong?", and agree on edge cases upfront. Second, **test case design during development**: instead of writing test cases after code was merged, I wrote them in parallel — which also meant I could flag ambiguities while the developer was still in context. Third, I encouraged developers to write unit tests for the logic I identified as high-risk during kick-off. I tracked where defects were being found month-over-month.

**Result:** Over three months, the percentage of defects found in system testing dropped from 68% to 41%. Defects found earlier (in kick-offs, code review, or unit tests) rose from 12% to 35%. Sprint velocity improved because fewer late-cycle surprises were disrupting the team.

## Key Terms

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Shift-Left Testing** | Moving testing activities earlier in the SDLC to prevent defects rather than detect them late. |
| **Story Kick-Off** | A brief pre-development session to align on requirements, edge cases, and testability. |
| **Three Amigos** | A collaborative refinement session involving developer, tester, and product owner. |
| **Defect Cost Curve** | The principle that defects become exponentially more expensive to fix the later they are discovered. |
| **Shift-Right Testing** | Complementary practice — testing in or near production to observe real user behaviour (e.g., canary releases, A/B testing, monitoring). |
| **Quality Gate** | An automated or manual checkpoint that enforces quality criteria before progression to the next phase. |
