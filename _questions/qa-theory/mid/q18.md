category: qa-theory

theory: "Testing is complete when predefined exit criteria have been met. These criteria typically include requirements coverage thresholds, defect closure rates, risk sign-off, and stakeholder acceptance. Because exhaustive testing is impossible, 'done' is always a risk-informed decision, not an absolute state."

## The Intent

The interviewer is testing your ability to make and defend a professional judgement call under real-world pressure. "We ran out of time" is not an acceptable answer. They want to hear about exit criteria, risk acceptance, and stakeholder communication — showing that you treat release readiness as a structured decision, not a feeling.

---

## The STAR Script

**Situation:** Two days before a scheduled release, I had 12 open defects — 2 critical, 4 major, and 6 minor. The business was pushing to release on schedule.

**Task:** I needed to make a clear, evidence-based recommendation on whether we were ready to release, and communicate it in a way that allowed the business to make an informed decision.

**Action:** I referred to the exit criteria we had agreed at the start of the sprint: zero open critical defects, no more than three open major defects, 95% of planned test cases executed, and product owner sign-off on residual risk. We failed on two criteria: 2 critical defects remained open and we had only executed 88% of planned cases due to an environment outage. I prepared a one-page release readiness report showing: criteria status (pass/fail), the nature of the critical defects and their user impact, the unexecuted test areas, and three options — delay release, release with a hotfix plan, or release with known risk documented. I presented this to the product owner and release manager.

**Result:** The team chose to delay by three days to resolve the critical defects. The remaining major and minor defects were accepted with a documented risk register. The release went out cleanly. The structured communication meant the delay was a business decision, not a QA blocker.

## Seniority Shift

| Level | Expected Depth |
|-------|---------------|
| **Entry** | Define exit criteria and list common examples (defect counts, coverage percentages). |
| **Mid** | Describe how to apply exit criteria in practice, handle pressure to release with open defects, and communicate release readiness clearly to non-technical stakeholders. |
| **Senior** | Build a release readiness framework for a team, define tiered exit criteria by risk level, and embed automated quality gates in CI/CD that enforce criteria without manual intervention. |

---

## Key Terms

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Exit Criteria** | Predefined conditions that must be satisfied before testing is considered complete. |
| **Release Readiness** | A structured assessment of whether a build is fit for production deployment. |
| **Defect Closure Rate** | The percentage of found defects that have been resolved and verified. |
| **Residual Risk** | Known, accepted risk that remains at the point of release. |
| **Risk Register** | A document recording known risks, their likelihood, impact, and acceptance status. |
| **Quality Gate** | A checkpoint in the pipeline that blocks progression if defined quality criteria are not met. |
