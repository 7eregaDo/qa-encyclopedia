## The Intent

The interviewer is testing your adaptability and professional maturity. Complaining that "requirements weren't clear" is a red flag. They want to see that you have practical strategies for maintaining quality in ambiguous conditions — because that is the reality of most projects.

---

## The STAR Script

**Situation:** Midway through a sprint, the product owner changed the acceptance criteria for a core filtering feature after development had already begun. Three of my existing test cases were now invalid, and two new scenarios had no specification at all.

**Task:** I needed to adapt quickly without letting coverage gaps slip through or blocking the sprint.

**Action:** I took four steps. First, I flagged the change immediately in the team standup so the impact was visible. Second, I updated and marked the invalidated test cases to prevent them from generating false failures. Third, for the two unspecified scenarios, I set up a 15-minute three-amigos session with the developer and product owner to define expected behaviour — turning ambiguity into testable acceptance criteria before I wrote a single test case. Fourth, I added a coverage delta note to the sprint test summary, documenting what changed and what was newly tested vs. deferred.

**Result:** Both new scenarios were clarified and tested within the same sprint. The sprint shipped on time with no ambiguity-related defects reaching UAT. The three-amigos approach became a recurring practice for any mid-sprint requirement changes.

## Seniority Shift

| Level | Expected Depth |
|-------|---------------|
| **Entry** | Describe how you'd communicate the impact of changing requirements and ask clarifying questions. |
| **Mid** | Demonstrate active strategies: three-amigos sessions, exploratory testing for undefined areas, and impact assessment of requirement changes on existing test coverage. |
| **Senior** | Discuss how to build a resilient test architecture (data-driven tests, abstracted page objects) that minimises rework when requirements change, and how to report coverage confidence to stakeholders under uncertainty. |

## Key Terms

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Acceptance Criteria** | Conditions a feature must meet to be considered complete and testable. |
| **Three Amigos** | A collaborative session between a developer, tester, and product owner to clarify a story before development begins. |
| **Exploratory Testing** | Unscripted testing used to investigate undefined or ambiguous areas. |
| **Impact Analysis** | Assessing which existing test cases are affected by a requirement change. |
| **Coverage Delta** | The difference in test coverage between the original plan and the current state after changes. |
| **Definition of Ready** | A checklist confirming a user story is sufficiently defined before it enters a sprint. |
