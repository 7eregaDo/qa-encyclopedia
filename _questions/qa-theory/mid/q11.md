## The Intent

The interviewer wants to see that you can make strategic decisions under time pressure — not just execute a test plan mechanically. Mid-level engineers are expected to own prioritisation decisions and justify them clearly to stakeholders. This question separates testers who think from testers who just execute.

---

## The STAR Script

**Situation:** On a major release with a two-week testing window, we had 400+ test cases and a mid-sprint scope expansion that added three new features. There was no way to execute everything.

**Task:** I needed to triage the test suite and build a prioritised execution plan that the team and product owner could align on.

**Action:** I created a simple risk matrix scoring each feature area on two dimensions: **likelihood of failure** (based on code churn, developer confidence, and complexity) and **business impact** (based on revenue exposure, user volume, and regulatory risk). The payment flow and user authentication scored highest on both axes and were designated as must-test. New features with partial spec were flagged as medium risk. Legacy, untouched modules were deprioritised. I documented the decisions and got explicit sign-off from the product owner on what was being deferred.

**Result:** We executed 280 of the 400 cases covering 95% of the risk surface. Three critical defects were found in the payment flow. The deferred areas were tested in the following sprint. No production incidents arose from the deferred scope.

## Key Terms

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Risk-Based Testing** | Prioritising test effort based on the probability and impact of failure. |
| **Risk Matrix** | A grid scoring areas by likelihood of failure and business impact. |
| **Likelihood** | The probability that a given area contains or introduces a defect. |
| **Impact** | The business or user consequence if a defect in this area reaches production. |
| **Residual Risk** | Risk that remains after testing is complete due to deferred or incomplete coverage. |
| **Risk Sign-Off** | Formal stakeholder acknowledgement of what is and isn't being tested before release. |
