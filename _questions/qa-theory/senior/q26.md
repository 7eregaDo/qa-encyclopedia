## The Intent

The interviewer is assessing leadership capability and organisational influence. This question is about culture change, not testing technique. They want to see that you can operate beyond your immediate team, influence people who don't report to you, and create lasting improvement rather than compliance with a process.

---

## The STAR Script

**Situation:** I joined an organisation where QA was seen as a blocker — the team that "stops releases" and "raises problems without solving them." Developers would argue defects were "not bugs," PMs would pressure QA to sign off early, and quality was treated as QA's problem alone.

**Task:** I needed to shift the cultural perception of quality from a QA responsibility to a shared engineering value — without authority over the people I needed to influence.

**Action:** I worked across four dimensions. **People**: I stopped being the sole defect reporter and started co-owning quality with developers. I introduced "quality champions" — a rotating role where developers participated in exploratory testing sessions each sprint. This built empathy for what QA does and gave developers skin in the game. **Process**: I moved quality conversations upstream by facilitating three-amigos sessions and making acceptance criteria review a default sprint ceremony. Quality became a planning topic, not just a release blocker. **Visibility**: I created a shared quality dashboard — visible to the whole team — showing escaped defects, test coverage trends, and production incident frequency. When the data was shared, teams self-corrected without being told to. **Recognition**: I worked with engineering managers to include quality contributions in performance reviews — developers who wrote good unit tests, caught bugs in review, or improved documentation were acknowledged alongside delivery velocity.

**Result:** Within six months, the percentage of defects raised by non-QA team members doubled. Developers began volunteering for exploratory sessions. The phrase "that's a QA problem" disappeared from retrospectives. Two years later, the organisation adopted "quality is everyone's responsibility" as a formal engineering principle.

## Key Terms

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Quality Culture** | An environment where quality is a shared value owned by the whole team, not a single function. |
| **Quality Champion** | A developer or team member who actively advocates for and participates in quality activities. |
| **Whole-Team Quality** | An Agile principle asserting that everyone on the team is responsible for quality. |
| **Psychological Safety** | An environment where people feel safe to raise quality concerns without blame or reprisal. |
| **Visibility** | Making quality metrics transparent to all team members to drive self-correction and shared ownership. |
| **Shift-Left Mindset** | The cultural belief that quality is built in from the start, not inspected in at the end. |
