## The Intent

The interviewer wants to know whether you see QA as an end-of-pipeline activity ("test when coding is done") or as an integrated, continuous function. The former is a red flag at any level. They are also assessing whether you understand how testing activities map to different SDLC phases.

---

## The STAR Script

**Situation:** On a previous project, testing was only starting after development was marked "complete," leading to a bottleneck and rushed releases with high defect rates.

**Task:** I proposed shifting QA left — introducing quality activities earlier in the cycle.

**Action:** I mapped out where QA could add value at each phase: during **requirements**, I participated in reviews to catch ambiguous or untestable requirements; during **design**, I reviewed architecture documents for testability concerns; during **development**, I wrote test cases in parallel with coding; during **testing**, I executed and reported; during **deployment**, I ran smoke tests; and after release, I monitored error logs and user feedback.

**Result:** Defects caught in requirements and design phases cost a fraction of those found post-development. The team saw a 30% reduction in bugs reaching UAT over the following quarter.

## Key Terms

| SDLC Phase | QA Activity |
|------------|-------------|
| **Requirements** | Requirements review, identifying ambiguities and untestable criteria. |
| **Design** | Architecture and design reviews for testability. |
| **Development** | Test case creation, unit test review, static analysis. |
| **Testing** | Test execution, defect reporting, regression testing. |
| **Deployment** | Smoke testing, environment validation. |
| **Maintenance** | Monitoring, defect triage, regression suite updates. |
| **Shift-Left Testing** | The practice of moving testing activities earlier in the SDLC to reduce defect cost. |
