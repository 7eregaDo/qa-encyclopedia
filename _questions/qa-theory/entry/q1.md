## The Intent

The interviewer is testing whether you understand that QA is not simply "finding bugs." They want to see that you grasp the proactive, systemic nature of QA versus the reactive, inspection-based nature of QC. Confusing the two is one of the most common entry-level mistakes.

---

## The STAR Script

**Situation:** During my studies, I worked on a small team project where we initially had no defined process — developers would finish a feature and hand it to me to "check."

**Task:** I realised bugs were being introduced repeatedly because there was no agreed coding standard or review step. I needed to address the root cause, not just the symptoms.

**Action:** I introduced a simple checklist for developers to follow before handoff (QA), while continuing to run structured test cases against each build (QC). The checklist covered naming conventions, input validation, and error handling.

**Result:** The number of bugs reaching me dropped significantly because the process itself was improved. This taught me first-hand that QA prevents defects upstream, while QC catches them downstream.

## Key Terms

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Quality Assurance (QA)** | Process improvement activities aimed at preventing defects before they occur. |
| **Quality Control (QC)** | Inspection and testing activities aimed at detecting defects in the product. |
| **Defect Prevention** | Any activity that reduces the likelihood of defects being introduced. |
| **Defect Detection** | Any activity that finds defects already present in the product. |
| **Process Audit** | A QA activity that reviews whether teams are following agreed processes. |
