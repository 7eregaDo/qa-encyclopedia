## The Intent
The interviewer is checking whether you think strategically about the people dimension of your function. At lead level, the quality of your team determines the quality of everything else — and in data and AI, the talent market is competitive, the skill set is genuinely rare, and the wrong hiring decisions are costly to reverse.

## The STAR Script
**Situation:** Following approval for a six-person data and AI quality engineering team, I needed to hire four engineers (two were internal transfers). The talent market was extremely competitive — the skills I needed overlapped with data engineering, data science, and ML engineering, all of which commanded higher base salaries than traditional QA roles. I also needed to retain the two internal transfers, both of whom had received external offers within three months of joining.

**Task:** I needed to define a compelling hiring and retention proposition, attract candidates with the right profile, and build a team culture that made people want to stay.

**Action:** On hiring, I made three deliberate profile decisions. First, I hired for analytical reasoning and intellectual curiosity over domain experience — I designed interview exercises around data interpretation problems and scenario-based reasoning, not tool knowledge. I explicitly recruited from non-traditional backgrounds: one hire came from an academic statistics background, one from clinical research, one from a data journalism career. Second, I positioned the role accurately in job adverts — "you will design evaluation frameworks for AI systems with real consequences, work with legal and ethics stakeholders, and influence how your organisation builds AI" — rather than the generic "test AI features" framing. Third, I matched compensation to data engineering bands, not traditional QA bands, making an explicit case to HR that the skill set commanded parity. On retention, I introduced three practices. Individual research time: 10% of each engineer's time was protected for self-directed learning — papers, tooling experiments, conference attendance. They owned their learning agenda. Public representation: I actively created opportunities for team members to present work externally — internal tech talks, conference CFPs, blog posts. External recognition was a retention lever. Growth through ownership: within six months, each engineer owned a specific domain — one owned the agentic AI testing framework, one owned the fairness programme, one owned the RAG evaluation toolkit. Ownership created mastery and mastery created retention.

**Result:** All four external hires accepted offers. Both internal transfers declined their competing offers and remained. In 18 months of operation, the team had zero attrition. Three team members had presented at external conferences. One had been promoted to staff engineer — the first QA engineer in the organisation's history to reach that level. The team's work was cited in two industry publications as leading practice. I attributed the retention to ownership and external recognition far more than compensation.

## Seniority Shift
| Level | Expected Depth |
|-------|---------------|
| **Entry** | Is developing the skills and curiosity that make them a strong candidate for a data and AI quality role |
| **Mid** | Contributes to hiring decisions, helps onboard new team members, and mentors juniors in the domain |
| **Senior** | Owns hiring decisions for their domain, designs the interview process, and builds team capability through coaching and structured learning |
| **Lead** | Defines the team's hiring philosophy, career pathway, and culture — building a team that attracts talent through its reputation and retains it through meaningful work and genuine growth |

## Key Terms
| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Hiring profile** | A defined set of attributes, skills, and values that characterise the ideal candidate — used to ensure consistency and intentionality across hiring decisions |
| **Non-traditional background** | Candidates whose career history is outside conventional QA pathways — valuable for bringing domain knowledge, analytical rigour, and fresh perspectives to a specialist discipline |
| **Research time** | Protected time for self-directed learning — a retention mechanism that signals the organisation values engineers as thinkers, not only as producers |
| **Public representation** | Opportunities for team members to present their work at conferences, in publications, or in internal forums — building individual reputation and team visibility |
| **Domain ownership** | Assigning a specific area of the quality programme to an individual — creating mastery, accountability, and a clear identity within the team |
| **Staff engineer** | A senior individual contributor track that provides a growth path equivalent to management without requiring people management responsibilities |
| **Compensation parity** | Matching salaries to the relevant talent market (data engineering, ML engineering) rather than to historical QA compensation bands — necessary for competing for specialist talent |
