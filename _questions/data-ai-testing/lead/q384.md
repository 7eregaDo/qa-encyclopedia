## The Intent
The interviewer is checking whether you have genuine strategic influence — shaping what AI gets built and how, not just whether it passes a gate. At lead level, the most impactful quality work happens upstream of development, in the product strategy conversations where AI decisions are made.

## The STAR Script
**Situation:** My organisation's product leadership was considering three major AI initiatives for the following year: an AI-powered personalisation engine, an automated content moderation system, and a generative AI feature for customer-facing contract drafting. Each was sponsored by a different VP and had already been included in draft product roadmaps. I had been asked to provide a "quality perspective" before the roadmap was finalised — which I understood as an opportunity to influence the sequencing and scope, not just provide a risk warning.

**Task:** I needed to assess each initiative's quality and risk profile and present findings in a form that would shape investment decisions, not just inform them.

**Action:** I produced a one-page risk and quality profile for each initiative, structured around four dimensions: technical readiness (did the organisation have the data quality, tooling, and expertise to build this well?), regulatory exposure (what were the compliance obligations and their cost?), quality investment required (what would it take to reach production-ready quality?), and precedent risk (if this went wrong publicly, what was the reputational consequence?). Personalisation engine: moderate technical readiness, low regulatory exposure, straightforward quality investment — green. Automated content moderation: low technical readiness (our training data was insufficient for the cultural nuance required), high regulatory exposure (the DSA in the EU imposed specific accuracy obligations for content moderation at scale), high quality investment — amber with conditions. Generative contract drafting: moderate technical readiness, very high regulatory and legal exposure (contracts with legal effect drafted by an AI without a qualified human review step created significant liability), extreme precedent risk — red without a human-in-the-loop architecture. I presented the profiles to the CPO alongside concrete recommendations: proceed with personalisation in Q1, proceed with content moderation in Q2 with a 6-week data quality sprint first, and redesign the contract drafting feature with mandatory lawyer review before AI output is presented to the customer.

**Result:** The CPO accepted all three recommendations. The contract drafting feature was redesigned — the AI drafted, a lawyer reviewed, the customer saw the final version — reducing liability exposure while preserving the efficiency gain. The content moderation sprint was funded and completed, improving training data quality before development began. The personalisation engine launched on schedule. I was added as a standing member of the product strategy forum for AI initiatives — the first time a quality role had a seat in that forum.

## Seniority Shift
| Level | Expected Depth |
|-------|---------------|
| **Entry** | Understands that quality considerations should inform product decisions, not just validate them |
| **Mid** | Flags quality and regulatory risks during feature design with specific recommendations |
| **Senior** | Proactively profiles AI initiatives for quality risk and presents findings to engineering and product leadership before development begins |
| **Lead** | Influences portfolio-level AI investment decisions by translating quality data into business risk and opportunity framing — earning a standing role in product strategy as a trusted executive partner |

## Key Terms
| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Risk and quality profile** | A structured one-page summary of an AI initiative's technical readiness, regulatory exposure, quality investment requirement, and precedent risk — designed for executive decision-making |
| **DSA (Digital Services Act)** | EU regulation imposing transparency, accuracy, and redress obligations on platforms operating content recommendation and moderation systems at scale |
| **Human-in-the-loop** | An AI architecture requiring a human to review or approve model outputs before they have real-world effect — reducing liability and improving safety for high-stakes applications |
| **Precedent risk** | The reputational and legal consequence of being the first prominent organisation to experience a specific AI failure publicly — relevant to novel or high-visibility use cases |
| **Technical readiness** | An assessment of whether an organisation has the data quality, tooling, expertise, and infrastructure required to build an AI system to production quality standards |
| **Upstream influence** | Shaping decisions before they are made — in AI quality, participating in product strategy before development begins rather than validating what has already been built |
| **Standing member** | A consistent presence in a recurring decision-making forum — the organisational recognition that quality expertise is a strategic input to AI product decisions, not a post-hoc review |
