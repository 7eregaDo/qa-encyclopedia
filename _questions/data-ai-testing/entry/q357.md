## The Intent
The interviewer is checking whether you understand that AI quality includes fairness, not only accuracy. A model that is 90% accurate overall but 60% accurate for a specific demographic group is not a good model — and QA engineers who do not test for this can ship systems that cause real harm.

## The STAR Script
**Situation:** Our team was building a CV screening tool that used an ML model to rank job applicants by predicted suitability. Before launch, I raised the question of whether the model had been tested for fairness across candidate groups.

**Task:** The team had no existing fairness test approach. I was asked to propose a basic bias assessment that could be completed before the launch deadline.

**Action:** I identified three tests achievable with the data available. First, performance parity: I split the labelled test set by gender (where declared) and measured the model's accuracy separately for each group. I found the model was 12 percentage points less accurate for female candidates in technical roles — a meaningful disparity. Second, output distribution analysis: I examined the score distribution for candidates grouped by gender and found that female candidates with equivalent stated qualifications to male candidates received statistically lower ranking scores. Third, proxy variable check: I reviewed the model's feature importances and found that "gap in employment history" was the third most influential feature — a variable that disproportionately disadvantages candidates who have taken career breaks for caregiving, which correlates with gender. I documented all three findings with the data and flagged them as launch blockers.

**Result:** The launch was delayed by three weeks while the training data was rebalanced and the gap feature was removed. The model's accuracy parity improved to within 3 percentage points. The organisation's legal team confirmed the original model would have created material legal risk under employment law. The bias assessment was added to the standard launch checklist for all ML features.

## Key Terms
| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **AI bias** | A systematic tendency in a model's outputs to favour or disadvantage particular groups, resulting in inequitable outcomes |
| **Performance parity** | A fairness criterion requiring that a model's accuracy or error rate is equivalent across demographic groups |
| **Proxy variable** | A feature that correlates with a protected characteristic (gender, ethnicity, age) and can introduce indirect discrimination even when the protected characteristic is not explicitly used |
| **Feature importance** | A measure of how much each input feature contributed to a model's predictions — used to identify potentially problematic variables |
| **Protected characteristic** | An attribute (gender, race, age, disability, etc.) that is legally or ethically protected against discrimination in decision-making |
| **Output distribution analysis** | Examining the statistical distribution of model scores or predictions across different groups to identify systematic differences |
| **Training data bias** | Bias introduced through unrepresentative, historically skewed, or label-noisy training data — the most common root cause of AI bias |
