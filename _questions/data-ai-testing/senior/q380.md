## The Intent
The interviewer is checking whether you operate as a strategic partner to data science, not just a downstream gatekeeper. At senior level, the most impactful QA contribution is changing how models are built, not just whether they pass a gate — and that requires technical credibility, relationship, and the ability to frame findings as improvement opportunities.

## The STAR Script
**Situation:** Over 18 months of evaluating ML models at my organisation, I had accumulated a pattern of recurring findings across different models from the same data science team: fairness gaps on minority demographic groups, evaluation metrics that looked strong but masked poor performance on edge cases, and production drift within 8–12 weeks of launch. Each finding was fixed individually, but the patterns kept recurring because the root causes were in the team's development workflow, not in any specific model.

**Task:** I wanted to translate the accumulated findings into a set of concrete workflow changes that would address the root causes rather than the symptoms.

**Action:** I prepared a findings synthesis document — a one-page summary of the three recurring patterns, each with the data from three or four model evaluations to show it was a pattern, not an isolated incident. I requested a working session with the data science lead (not a QA review — a peer consultation). In the session, I presented the patterns with specific questions rather than prescriptions: "I've noticed fairness gaps appearing consistently in models trained on pre-2021 data — what's your current process for checking training data representativeness before training begins?" The conversation surfaced three workflow gaps: no pre-training data quality report, evaluation metrics chosen after seeing the data rather than pre-specified, and no post-launch monitoring plan included in the model development brief. I proposed three specific additions to the team's model development workflow: a mandatory pre-training data quality and representativeness report (I offered to write the template), pre-registration of primary and fairness metrics before any model is trained (borrowed from clinical trial methodology), and a monitoring plan as a required section of the model release document. I framed each as reducing rework — the team was spending significant time on post-hoc fixes that these steps would prevent.

**Result:** The data science lead adopted all three additions to the development workflow. The first model developed under the new process had no fairness findings at evaluation — the pre-training data check had caught a representativeness gap before training began. The data science lead cited the framing ("reducing rework") as the reason the proposal landed — it was positioned as helping them, not policing them.

## Seniority Shift
| Level | Expected Depth |
|-------|---------------|
| **Entry** | Can describe what they found in testing and hand findings to a data scientist clearly |
| **Mid** | Translates evaluation findings into specific root cause hypotheses and proposes remediation approaches for individual models |
| **Senior** | Synthesises patterns across multiple models, identifies systemic workflow gaps, and influences data science development practices through peer-level consultation and concrete workflow proposals |

## Key Terms
| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Findings synthesis** | The aggregation of individual test findings across multiple engagements to identify systemic patterns — a higher-order analytical output than per-model reports |
| **Root cause** | The underlying workflow, data, or methodological issue that produces a recurring defect — addressing it prevents recurrence rather than just fixing the symptom |
| **Pre-registration** | Declaring evaluation metrics and hypotheses before any model training begins — preventing post-hoc selection of metrics that make a model look better than it is |
| **Pre-training data quality report** | A structured assessment of training data representativeness, quality, and potential bias conducted before model training starts |
| **Peer consultation** | A collaborative conversation between equals — distinct from a review or audit — more likely to produce genuine workflow change because it respects the data scientist's expertise |
| **Rework framing** | Positioning a quality practice as reducing downstream correction effort rather than as an external compliance burden — the most effective framing for influencing engineering workflows |
| **Systemic improvement** | A change to a workflow, process, or standard that prevents a class of defect from recurring — as opposed to fixing individual instances |
