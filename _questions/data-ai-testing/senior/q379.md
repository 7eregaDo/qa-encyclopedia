## The Intent
The interviewer is checking whether you can grow a team's capability strategically, not just personally develop your own. At senior level, your impact is multiplied through the people you develop — and data and AI testing is a domain where the knowledge gap in most QA teams is significant.

## The STAR Script
**Situation:** I was the only QA engineer in my organisation with data and AI testing experience. The four other QA engineers on my team had strong functional and automation testing backgrounds but had never tested a data pipeline or an ML model. The organisation was rapidly expanding its AI feature portfolio and the team needed to support it.

**Task:** I needed to build the team's collective capability without creating a bottleneck where all data and AI work routed through me.

**Action:** I designed a capability-building programme in three stages. Stage 1 — foundation knowledge (months 1–2): I identified the minimum viable knowledge set — data quality dimensions, what a pipeline is, what a model is, why probabilistic outputs need statistical evaluation — and ran four 90-minute internal workshops, one per topic, with hands-on exercises. I connected each topic to something the engineers already knew: data quality checks are assertions just like unit test assertions; model evaluation metrics are precision/recall which they had encountered in search relevance testing. Making the unfamiliar familiar reduced the perceived learning cliff significantly. Stage 2 — embedded practice (months 3–5): I paired each QA engineer with a data or AI squad for one sprint, with a specific deliverable: write one expectation suite for an existing pipeline, or add a labelled test set evaluation to an existing model. I ran weekly 30-minute debrief sessions where each engineer shared one thing they had learned and one question they could not answer — the questions became the agenda for the following week's knowledge-sharing. Stage 3 — shared artefacts (ongoing): I built a data and AI testing starter kit — a Great Expectations template, a dbt test checklist, a model evaluation report template, and a safety test playbook — that any engineer could pick up without prior knowledge. I also wrote a decision tree: given this type of AI feature, use this testing approach. The starter kit reduced the time from "assigned to a data/AI ticket" to "first meaningful test written" from days to under two hours.

**Result:** Within six months, all four engineers had independently led at least one data or AI testing engagement without my involvement. Two had become the primary QA contacts for specific ML squads. The starter kit was adopted by three other QA teams in the organisation after being shared internally. I had moved from being a single point of failure to being one of five capable engineers.

## Seniority Shift
| Level | Expected Depth |
|-------|---------------|
| **Entry** | Is developing their own data and AI testing skills and can explain what they are learning and why |
| **Mid** | Can onboard a colleague into basic data and AI testing practices and share knowledge through documentation |
| **Senior** | Designs and leads a structured capability-building programme — knowledge foundation, embedded practice, shared artefacts — that develops a full team's data and AI testing capability |

## Key Terms
| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Capability building** | The systematic development of skills, knowledge, and practices within a team — distinct from individual learning |
| **Transferable skills** | Existing competencies (e.g., assertion writing, test design) that apply in a new domain with domain-specific adaptation |
| **Embedded practice** | A learning approach where engineers build knowledge through real project work in a new domain, with structured reflection — more durable than classroom training |
| **Starter kit** | A pre-built, documented set of templates and tools enabling a team member to begin productive work in a new domain with minimal ramp-up time |
| **Single point of failure** | A situation where critical knowledge or capability resides with one individual — an organisational risk that capability-building programmes are designed to eliminate |
| **Knowledge-sharing ritual** | A recurring lightweight session where team members share recent learnings — sustaining capability growth and surfacing knowledge gaps continuously |
| **Decision tree** | A structured guide that maps a situation (type of AI feature) to a recommended approach — reducing cognitive load for engineers working in a new domain |
