## The Intent
The interviewer is checking whether you can operate effectively across disciplines that have different vocabularies, tools, and incentives. Data and AI quality cannot be owned by QA in isolation — it requires buy-in and active participation from data scientists and engineers who may not have a testing background.

## The STAR Script
**Situation:** I was the QA lead on a cross-functional squad comprising two data scientists, two data engineers, and a product manager. Data quality had historically been treated as the data engineers' problem — the data scientists focused on model accuracy, the product manager on feature delivery, and nobody had a shared definition of "quality" or a shared process for maintaining it.

**Task:** I wanted to establish a cross-functional quality practice that gave each role a meaningful stake in data and AI quality without adding friction to their primary work.

**Action:** I ran three separate conversations before proposing any process change. With the data engineers: I asked what broke most often and what tests they wished existed — they named schema changes from upstream APIs as their biggest pain point. I offered to help write contract tests and own the schema monitoring alerting. With the data scientists: I asked what model failures had been most painful to diagnose in the past year — they described a case where training data quality issues had caused a model to perform well in evaluation but poorly in production. I offered to introduce a training data quality report as part of the model training workflow. With the product manager: I asked what user behaviour signals indicated quality problems — she mentioned an uptick in "wrong recommendation" feedback through the in-app thumbs-down button. I offered to set up a dashboard connecting that signal to model confidence scores. I then proposed a lightweight shared quality ritual: a fortnightly 20-minute quality check-in where each role brought one quality signal from their domain. No action items required — just shared visibility.

**Result:** The fortnightly ritual surfaced a training data issue within its first three sessions: the data scientists noticed that the contract test alerts I had implemented were catching upstream schema changes that were subtly corrupting the training dataset they had been treating as reliable. The connection would not have been made without the cross-disciplinary conversation. The product manager began citing the thumbs-down/confidence score dashboard in sprint reviews, making model quality visible at a business level.

## Seniority Shift
| Level | Expected Depth |
|-------|---------------|
| **Entry** | Can work within a cross-functional data team and contribute to shared quality practices |
| **Mid** | Initiates and facilitates cross-functional quality conversations, adapts quality framing to each stakeholder's language and concerns, and builds shared rituals that create collective ownership |
| **Senior** | Designs the cross-functional quality operating model for a data and AI programme — roles, responsibilities, shared processes, and escalation paths — and coaches other QA engineers to operate effectively in data contexts |

## Key Terms
| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Cross-functional squad** | A team combining members from different disciplines (engineering, data science, product) working toward a shared outcome |
| **Stakeholder language** | The vocabulary, priorities, and mental models of a specific role — quality concerns land differently when framed in terms each stakeholder finds meaningful |
| **Training data quality** | The accuracy, completeness, and representativeness of the data used to train a machine learning model — a determinant of model quality that is upstream of model evaluation |
| **In-app feedback signal** | User-generated quality signal (thumbs down, report button) that can be used as a proxy for model output quality in production |
| **Quality ritual** | A recurring, lightweight team practice that creates shared visibility of quality signals — more sustainable than one-off reviews |
| **Shared ownership** | A team dynamic where quality is treated as everyone's responsibility rather than a single role's obligation — a necessary condition for sustained quality in data and AI systems |
| **Model confidence score** | A model's self-reported probability that its prediction is correct — useful as a production quality proxy when ground-truth labels are not immediately available |
