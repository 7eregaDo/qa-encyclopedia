## The Intent
The interviewer is checking whether you understand that deploying a model is not the end of QA — it is the beginning of a new testing context. At mid level, they want to see that you can design and implement the monitoring that makes the model's ongoing quality visible.

## The STAR Script
**Situation:** Our product recommendation model had passed pre-launch evaluation with 87% accuracy on the test set. Three months after launch, the product team noticed click-through rates on recommendations had declined by 22% over six weeks. No monitoring was in place and no one could explain why.

**Task:** I was asked to investigate the decline and implement monitoring that would detect similar degradation early in future.

**Action:** I investigated retrospectively using logged input and output data. Input drift: I compared the distribution of user feature values (recency of last purchase, product category affinity scores) between the training period and the current period. Recency scores had shifted significantly — a large cohort of previously active users had become dormant, changing the input distribution the model had never been trained on. Output drift: the model's confidence scores had been declining steadily — the proportion of predictions above 0.8 confidence had dropped from 71% to 38%, visible in the logs but unmonitored. Business metric: click-through rate had started declining three weeks before the product team noticed — had we been monitoring it with an alert threshold, we would have had a three-week head start. I then implemented four monitors. First, a weekly feature distribution check using Evidently AI — PSI (Population Stability Index) alert when any key feature drifted beyond 0.2. Second, a daily confidence score distribution monitor — alert when the proportion of high-confidence predictions dropped below 60%. Third, a business metric alert — click-through rate below 4% (versus a 6% baseline) triggered a review. Fourth, a monthly accuracy audit on a human-labelled sample of 200 recent predictions.

**Result:** The model was retrained on recent data. Click-through rates recovered within two weeks of the new model deploying. The PSI monitor detected a further feature drift event four months later — this time caught two weeks before business metrics were affected.

## Seniority Shift
| Level | Expected Depth |
|-------|---------------|
| **Entry** | Understands that models can degrade in production and that monitoring is needed |
| **Mid** | Implements production monitors for input drift, output drift, confidence scores, and business metrics — with defined alert thresholds |
| **Senior** | Designs the full model monitoring programme — tooling, alert routing, retraining triggers, rollback procedures — and connects monitoring signals to the model lifecycle governance process |

## Key Terms
| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **PSI (Population Stability Index)** | A metric measuring the shift in a feature's distribution between two time periods — values above 0.2 typically indicate significant drift |
| **Input drift** | A change in the statistical distribution of model input features in production compared to the training period |
| **Output drift** | A change in the distribution of model predictions over time — a leading indicator of model degradation even before ground truth labels are available |
| **Confidence score** | A model's self-reported probability that its prediction is correct — declining confidence scores can signal that the model is encountering unfamiliar input patterns |
| **Business metric** | A downstream outcome measure (click-through rate, conversion, fraud rate) that reflects the real-world impact of model quality |
| **Accuracy audit** | A periodic evaluation of model predictions against human-labelled ground truth, used to measure actual accuracy in production |
| **Retraining trigger** | A defined condition (drift threshold breach, accuracy audit failure, business metric alert) that initiates a model retraining cycle |
