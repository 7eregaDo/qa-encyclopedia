## The Intent
The interviewer is assessing your ability to communicate up — a defining lead competency. Technically excellent QE programmes fail to receive investment if the people controlling budgets can't understand their value. They want to see that you can translate API quality work into business terms, tell a story with data, and answer the executive question: "are we getting better, and does it matter?"

## The STAR Script
**Situation:** As Director of QE, I ran a comprehensive API quality programme — contract testing, security scanning, synthetic monitoring — but the CTO and CPO had little visibility into it. When API-related incidents occurred, the reaction was reactive surprise rather than informed risk management. In the annual budget cycle, QE infrastructure investment was consistently deprioritised because leadership had no clear picture of its value.

**Task:** Design an executive reporting approach that made API quality legible to non-technical stakeholders and that supported budget and prioritisation decisions.

**Action:** I built a quarterly API quality report structured around three business questions, not technical metrics. "Are our APIs reliable for consumers?" — I reported consumer-facing uptime per API (not internal test pass rates), mean time to detect API incidents, and the number of consumer-impacting incidents in the quarter with a trend line. "Are we getting safer?" — I reported the number of high/critical security findings found and remediated, with the trend over four quarters. I deliberately framed this as "found and fixed" rather than just "found" to prevent the report from looking like a wall of bad news. "Are we delivering faster without sacrificing quality?" — I reported deployment frequency for API changes alongside change failure rate and mean time to restore. These were DORA metrics translated into a business narrative: "we deployed 40% more frequently than last quarter while reducing the change failure rate from 8% to 3%." I presented this as a one-page PDF with three traffic-light indicators and supporting charts — nothing requiring technical interpretation. I presented it in the monthly engineering leadership review and made it available to the CPO before quarterly planning. I also included a "what's at risk" section — two or three APIs with degraded metrics and what investment was needed to address them.

**Result:** The next budget cycle included a line item for API infrastructure investment — the first time QE platform work had been explicitly funded. The CTO referenced the report in a board presentation as evidence of engineering quality maturity. The CPO began using the risk section in product planning to sequence API reliability work alongside feature delivery.

## Seniority Shift
| Level | Expected Depth |
|-------|---------------|
| **Entry** | Reports test results to their squad; not involved in stakeholder communication |
| **Mid** | Presents quality metrics in sprint reviews; can summarise API test results for non-technical team members |
| **Senior** | Produces quality dashboards and trend reports for engineering leadership; frames technical findings in risk terms |
| **Lead** | Designs executive-level API quality reporting in business language, presents to CTO/CPO, uses reports to drive investment decisions, and frames quality as a business capability rather than a technical function |

## Key Terms
| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Executive Quality Report** | A periodic summary of quality metrics framed in business language — reliability, risk, and velocity — designed for non-technical stakeholders |
| **Consumer-Facing Uptime** | The availability of an API as experienced by its consumers, expressed as a percentage, distinct from infrastructure uptime metrics |
| **Change Failure Rate** | The proportion of deployments that result in a degradation requiring remediation, one of the four DORA metrics used to measure delivery quality |
| **Mean Time to Detect (MTTD)** | The average time between an API quality issue occurring and the team becoming aware of it, used as a measure of monitoring effectiveness |
| **Traffic-Light Indicator** | A visual reporting convention using red/amber/green status to communicate metric health at a glance to non-technical audiences |
| **Risk Section** | A component of an executive quality report that identifies specific APIs or areas with degraded quality metrics and the investment required to address them |
| **DORA Metrics** | Four key software delivery performance metrics (deployment frequency, lead time, change failure rate, mean time to restore) that connect engineering practices to business outcomes |
| **Quality Maturity** | The degree to which an organisation's quality practices are systematic, measurable, and continuously improving — a concept used in executive conversations to frame QE investment |
