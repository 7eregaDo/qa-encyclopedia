## The Intent
The interviewer wants to see whether you think of QE as an enabling function rather than a service function. At lead level, you should be building platforms that multiply the effectiveness of every engineer in the organisation, not acting as a centralised testing team that others depend on. This question probes product thinking applied to internal tooling.

## The STAR Script
**Situation:** At a company with 15 product squads, the central QE team of six was a constant bottleneck. Squads requested test environment provisioning, data setup assistance, and pipeline help as tickets to the QE team. The average wait time was three days. As a result, testing was treated as a late-stage activity and QEs were seen as a necessary overhead rather than an accelerant.

**Task:** Transform the QE team's operating model from a service desk into a platform team, so that product squads could self-serve quality infrastructure without raising a ticket.

**Action:** I mapped the most frequent requests and categorised them into four capability areas: environment provisioning, test data, pipeline templates, and observability access. For each, I designed a self-service interface. Environment provisioning became a CLI tool wrapping our Kubernetes and Helm setup — a squad ran one command to spin up an isolated environment seeded with their service's dependencies. Test data became a web-accessible factory API — squads called HTTP endpoints to provision entities (users, orders, products) in any environment without knowing the underlying data model. Pipeline templates became a shared GitHub Actions template library — squads referenced a template by name and got a compliant pipeline with quality gates pre-wired. Observability access became a self-serve Grafana workspace provisioner — squads clicked a button in the internal developer portal to get a dashboard scaffolded for their service. I ran quarterly "platform office hours" where any squad could ask questions or request new capabilities, which fed the platform roadmap. I also defined and published SLAs for each capability — e.g. environment spin-up in under three minutes.

**Result:** Ticket volume to the QE team dropped by 85% within two quarters. Squads reported in the engineering survey that quality tooling was now a strength rather than a blocker. The QE team shifted the majority of their time from request fulfilment to platform development and quality coaching — work with significantly higher leverage.

## Seniority Shift
| Level | Expected Depth |
|-------|---------------|
| **Entry** | Uses quality tooling provided by others; may raise requests when tooling is missing |
| **Mid** | Contributes to shared tooling; documents how to use existing quality infrastructure |
| **Senior** | Builds reusable quality components for their service area; advocates for platform investment |
| **Lead** | Designs and owns the quality platform product, defines self-service interfaces for all major capability areas, publishes SLAs, and shifts the team's operating model from service to platform |

## Key Terms
| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Self-Service Quality Platform** | An internal product that provides product squads with on-demand access to quality infrastructure — environments, data, pipelines, observability — without requiring assistance from a central team |
| **Internal Developer Portal** | A centralised web interface giving engineers access to documentation, self-service tooling, and operational capabilities for the systems they build and run |
| **Platform Team** | A team that builds and maintains shared infrastructure and tooling consumed by product squads, operating with a product mindset and defined SLAs |
| **Developer Experience (DX)** | The quality of the environment and tooling that engineers use to build, test, and deploy software, treated as a product with its own user research and iteration |
| **CLI Tool** | A command-line interface utility that wraps complex infrastructure operations behind simple, discoverable commands for developer self-service |
| **Factory API** | A test-infrastructure service that provisions realistic, valid test data entities on demand via HTTP calls, abstracting the underlying data model from consumers |
| **Platform Roadmap** | A prioritised backlog of capabilities and improvements planned for a shared platform, informed by consumer feedback and strategic quality objectives |
| **Platform SLA** | A published service level agreement for a quality platform capability — such as environment spin-up time — that sets expectations and creates accountability for the platform team |
