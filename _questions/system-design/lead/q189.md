## The Intent
The interviewer wants to know whether you operate as a peer at the leadership table or as a technical implementer who is consulted at the end. This question is about influence, timing, and the ability to make quality a proactive input to business decisions rather than a reactive constraint on them.

## The STAR Script
**Situation:** At a scale-up preparing for rapid international expansion, quality conversations happened at the end of sprint reviews — "are we ready to release?" — rather than at the beginning of planning. Technical quality debt was accumulating because the product roadmap had no visibility into it. Two major launches had slipped because quality issues surfaced too late to address without delaying the release.

**Task:** As Director of QE, integrate quality as an upstream input to product and engineering planning so that quality risk was visible before commitments were made, not after.

**Action:** I introduced quality integration at three planning horizons. Annual OKR planning: I prepared a quality risk landscape document — a one-page assessment of the highest-risk areas of the system mapped against the planned product bets for the year. This gave the VP Product concrete information about which planned features would require quality investment as a prerequisite. It was presented in the annual planning offsite and resulted in quality infrastructure being included as a named workstream in the engineering OKRs for the first time. Quarterly planning: I introduced a "quality readiness brief" — a two-page input to the quarterly planning process covering the current state of the quality metrics, any tech debt that would affect the planned quarter's deliverables, and a capacity request for quality infrastructure work. Sprint planning: I worked with squad leads to include quality tasks — test coverage for new features, flakiness remediation, contract test additions — as first-class backlog items estimated and committed to alongside feature work, not added as an afterthought. I also established a release readiness review for major releases — a structured 30-minute session two weeks before a target release date where quality metrics, open defects, and risk items were reviewed with product and engineering leadership together.

**Result:** The two subsequent major international launches shipped on time. The annual quality risk landscape became a standing document requested by the CPO before planning. Quality infrastructure work received 15% of engineering capacity in the following annual plan — the first time it had been explicitly allocated.

## Seniority Shift
| Level | Expected Depth |
|-------|-------|
| **Entry** | Participates in sprint planning by estimating test tasks; not involved in higher-level planning |
| **Mid** | Advocates for quality tasks in sprint backlog; flags quality risks to the squad lead before planning |
| **Senior** | Contributes quality risk assessment to quarterly planning; participates in release readiness reviews |
| **Lead** | Integrates quality strategy into all three planning horizons, presents quality risk landscapes to executive leadership, secures explicit quality infrastructure capacity in annual plans, and owns the release readiness review process |

## Key Terms
| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Quality Risk Landscape** | An executive-level document mapping the highest-risk areas of a system against planned product initiatives, used to surface quality investment needs before roadmap commitments are made |
| **Quality Readiness Brief** | A structured input to quarterly planning that summarises quality metric status, tech debt relevant to the quarter's deliverables, and QE capacity requests |
| **Release Readiness Review** | A structured pre-release meeting that reviews quality metrics, open defects, and risk items with product and engineering leadership, producing a go/no-go decision |
| **Planning Horizon** | A timescale at which planning occurs — typically annual (OKRs), quarterly (sprint planning cycles), and sprint (two-week iterations) — each requiring different quality inputs |
| **Quality Infrastructure Capacity** | An explicit allocation of engineering effort to quality tooling, platform improvements, and test debt remediation, separate from feature delivery capacity |
| **Tech Debt Visibility** | The degree to which technical quality debt is represented in planning artefacts and understood by product and engineering leadership as a factor affecting delivery |
| **Release Commitment** | A public or internal commitment to ship a feature or product by a specific date, which quality strategy must inform before it is made rather than after |
| **OKR Cycle** | The annual or quarterly process of defining objectives and key results, which quality leads can influence to ensure quality metrics are included as shared team accountability |
