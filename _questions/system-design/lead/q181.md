## The Intent
The interviewer is probing whether you can think about quality as an organisational design problem, not just a technical one. At this scale, no individual can review every decision — the architecture must enforce quality structurally. They want to see executive-level thinking: how do you make good quality decisions the default, not the exception?

## The STAR Script
**Situation:** I joined a Series C company as Head of Quality Engineering when the engineering org was at 55 people across 8 teams. The CTO had a 12-month plan to triple headcount and double the number of product squads. The existing quality approach was informal — a handful of strong QEs whose institutional knowledge was the strategy.

**Task:** Establish a scalable quality architecture before the growth phase, so that new squads onboarded into a working system rather than a blank slate.

**Action:** I framed the vision around three principles: quality by default, quality by measurement, and quality by ownership. Quality by default meant the engineering platform embedded quality gates — new service templates included contract test scaffolding, structured logging configuration, and a baseline test suite out of the box. Squads couldn't easily skip these because the deployment pipeline required them. Quality by measurement meant I defined org-wide quality metrics — DORA metrics as leading indicators, defect escape rate and MTTD as lagging indicators — and made them visible in a shared engineering dashboard reviewed in the monthly engineering leadership meeting. Quality by ownership meant QEs were embedded in squads as quality coaches, not gatekeepers. I wrote a quality charter that defined what each squad owned versus what the central QE platform team owned, preventing both duplication and gaps. I presented this vision to the CTO and VP Engineering as a two-page architecture decision record, got explicit sign-off, and used it as the onboarding document for every new squad lead.

**Result:** By the time the org reached 160 engineers, 14 months later, new squads were productive in their quality practice within two weeks of formation. The defect escape rate remained flat despite the headcount tripling. The quality charter was cited in engineering all-hands as a model for other platform disciplines to follow.

## Seniority Shift
| Level | Expected Depth |
|-------|---------------|
| **Entry** | Focuses on individual test execution within a squad; unaware of cross-squad quality architecture |
| **Mid** | Advocates for quality standards within their squad; may influence one or two adjacent teams |
| **Senior** | Designs the quality strategy for a product area or set of services; defines standards and promotes them across squads |
| **Lead** | Establishes the org-wide quality architecture vision, secures executive alignment, embeds quality structurally into the engineering platform, and owns quality as an organisational capability |

## Key Terms
| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Quality Architecture** | The structural design of quality practices, tooling, and governance across an engineering organisation, ensuring testability and release confidence scale with team size |
| **Architecture Decision Record (ADR)** | A short document that captures a significant architectural decision, its context, the options considered, and the rationale for the choice made |
| **DORA Metrics** | Four key metrics (deployment frequency, lead time for changes, change failure rate, time to restore service) that measure the performance and stability of a software delivery process |
| **Defect Escape Rate** | The proportion of defects that reach production relative to the total defects found across all environments, used as a lagging quality indicator |
| **Quality Charter** | A document defining the responsibilities, standards, and boundaries of quality practice for squads and the central quality platform team |
| **Quality by Default** | A design principle in which quality gates, test scaffolding, and observability instrumentation are embedded in the engineering platform so teams adopt them without deliberate effort |
| **Engineering Platform** | The shared internal infrastructure, tooling, and service templates that product squads build on, maintained by a platform team to enforce consistency and reduce duplication |
| **Quality Coach** | An embedded QE role focused on raising a squad's quality capability through pairing, review, and education rather than performing QA on the squad's behalf |
