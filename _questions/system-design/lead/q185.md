## The Intent
The interviewer is assessing whether you can bridge the gap between quality philosophy and operational accountability. SLOs force precision — you have to define what quality means numerically, for whom, and within what time horizon. They want to know whether you've done this in practice and how you handled the organisational dynamics of holding teams to them.

## The STAR Script
**Situation:** At a B2C platform, quality was discussed in every planning meeting but measured differently by every team. The product team tracked customer complaints. Engineering tracked CI pass rates. QE tracked test coverage. Leadership had no unified view of quality and no shared language for prioritising quality work against feature work.

**Task:** As Director of Quality Engineering, establish a set of quality SLOs that gave leadership a single coherent view, and that teams could own and act on.

**Action:** I ran a facilitated workshop with the VP Engineering, VP Product, and four squad leads to define what quality meant from each perspective. We converged on five SLOs across two categories. Reliability SLOs: production error rate below 0.5% of requests (measured over 28 days), and p99 latency under 2 seconds for core user journeys (measured continuously). Delivery quality SLOs: defect escape rate below 3% (defects found in production as a proportion of all defects), CI pipeline pass rate above 95% (measured weekly), and mean time to restore below 30 minutes for P1 incidents. I defined each SLO with an explicit measurement methodology, a dashboard location, and a named owner at squad level. I then introduced an SLO review into the monthly engineering leadership meeting — a standing agenda item where any SLO in breach was discussed and a remediation owner assigned. I also defined error budgets: when a reliability SLO was consumed beyond its threshold, the affected squad paused new feature work and spent the remaining sprint on reliability improvements. This made quality trade-offs explicit rather than invisible.

**Result:** Within two quarters, leadership had a single quality dashboard referenced in board updates. The error budget mechanism triggered twice — both times the squads involved resolved the underlying issues within the sprint. Defect escape rate fell from 8% to 4.2% over the year as squads internalised the metric.

## Seniority Shift
| Level | Expected Depth |
|-------|---------------|
| **Entry** | Familiar with pass/fail metrics for individual tests; unaware of org-level quality measurement |
| **Mid** | Tracks quality metrics for their squad; can report on defect rates and coverage; flags breaches to senior staff |
| **Senior** | Defines quality metrics for a product area; introduces dashboards and review cadences; advocates for SLO thinking |
| **Lead** | Defines org-wide quality SLOs in collaboration with product and engineering leadership, implements error budgets, creates accountability mechanisms, and represents quality in executive and board-level reporting |

## Key Terms
| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **SLO (Service Level Objective)** | A specific, measurable target for a quality or reliability attribute of a service, agreed between engineering and product stakeholders |
| **Error Budget** | The acceptable amount of unreliability or quality failure implied by an SLO; when exhausted, it triggers a mandatory focus on reliability over feature delivery |
| **Defect Escape Rate** | The proportion of total defects that were not caught before reaching production, used as a lagging indicator of pre-production quality |
| **SLI (Service Level Indicator)** | The actual measured value of the attribute an SLO is defined against, such as the measured error rate over a 28-day window |
| **SLA (Service Level Agreement)** | A contractual or formal commitment to a customer or stakeholder about a service's quality or reliability, typically stricter and with consequences for breach |
| **Reliability SLO** | An SLO that defines acceptable thresholds for production reliability characteristics such as error rate, latency, or availability |
| **Delivery Quality SLO** | An SLO that defines acceptable thresholds for the engineering delivery process, such as defect escape rate, pipeline pass rate, or time to restore |
| **Error Budget Policy** | A defined rule that governs how a team responds when its error budget is consumed — typically mandating a pause on feature work in favour of reliability investment |
