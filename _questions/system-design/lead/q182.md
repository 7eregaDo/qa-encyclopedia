## The Intent
The interviewer wants to know whether you can operate at the executive layer — not just identify what needs to be built, but make the business case that gets it funded. This is a core lead competency: influencing without authority by speaking in the language of the audience.

## The STAR Script
**Situation:** At a growth-stage company, the test infrastructure had not kept pace with product growth. Flaky tests were costing approximately 90 minutes of engineer time per developer per week due to re-runs and investigation. The CI pipeline averaged 55 minutes. The VP Engineering wanted to address it but the CTO had deprioritised it twice in favour of feature work, citing lack of clear ROI.

**Task:** Build and present a business case to the CTO and CFO to secure a quarter of platform engineering time — roughly three engineers for 13 weeks — to overhaul the test infrastructure.

**Action:** I built the case in three sections. Cost of the status quo: I quantified the flakiness tax — 90 minutes per developer per week across 40 developers was 60 engineer-hours per week, or roughly 1.5 FTEs of lost productivity annually. At average fully-loaded cost, that was a six-figure annual loss. Pipeline duration was costing an estimated four hours of idle context-switching per developer per week — I cited research linking CI duration to merge frequency and developer satisfaction. Risk exposure: I pulled the incident log for the previous 12 months and identified four production incidents whose root cause was a gap in test coverage or a build process failure. I calculated the combined customer-facing downtime and support cost. Future state ROI: based on benchmarks from comparable investments at similar companies (sourced from the DORA State of DevOps report), I projected a 60% reduction in pipeline time and an 80% reduction in flakiness within two quarters, translating to a payback period of under five months. I presented this as a one-page executive summary with a supporting appendix, and offered two investment options — full scope and a reduced MVP — with different payback timelines.

**Result:** The CTO approved the full scope investment in the following planning cycle. The framing as a productivity and risk problem — not a quality problem — was the decisive factor. The CTO later referenced the model I used as the standard format for platform investment proposals across the engineering org.

## Seniority Shift
| Level | Expected Depth |
|-------|---------------|
| **Entry** | Raises quality concerns within the team; unlikely to engage with business case framing |
| **Mid** | Advocates for investment within engineering; can articulate the technical need clearly |
| **Senior** | Quantifies the cost of quality debt in engineering terms; influences prioritisation within their area |
| **Lead** | Builds a full executive business case with financial modelling, risk framing, and ROI projection; secures funding at the CTO/CFO level and establishes the investment model as an organisational standard |

## Key Terms
| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Business Case** | A structured argument for an investment that quantifies the cost of inaction, the expected return, and the risk of the proposed change, targeted at decision-makers |
| **Flakiness Tax** | The aggregate engineering time lost to investigating, re-running, and maintaining flaky tests, expressed as a productivity cost |
| **Fully-Loaded Cost** | The total cost of employing an engineer including salary, benefits, equipment, and overhead, used to convert engineer-hours into monetary terms |
| **Payback Period** | The time required for the financial benefits of an investment to equal its cost, used to assess the attractiveness of a capital allocation |
| **DORA State of DevOps Report** | An annual industry research report measuring software delivery performance across thousands of organisations, providing benchmarks for pipeline and delivery metrics |
| **Opportunity Cost** | The value of the next-best alternative foregone by choosing a particular course of action; used to argue that inaction has a cost, not just investment |
| **MVP (Minimum Viable Product)** | In an investment context, the smallest scope of work that delivers meaningful measurable value, used to offer a lower-risk alternative to full scope |
| **Platform Engineering Time** | Capacity allocated to the team responsible for shared developer tooling, CI/CD infrastructure, and test frameworks, as opposed to product feature delivery |
