## The Intent
The interviewer is testing your people leadership and team-building capability in a platform engineering context. Platform teams are notoriously difficult to lead well — they can easily become either an under-resourced bottleneck or an ivory tower building tools nobody uses. They want to see that you've thought about the team model deliberately and built something that other engineers valued.

## The STAR Script
**Situation:** I inherited a three-person "DevOps tools" team that was deeply reactive — their entire backlog was support tickets and one-off requests from product squads. They had no roadmap, no user research, and low morale. Experienced engineers on the team felt like a helpdesk rather than an engineering team. Two of the three were considering leaving.

**Task:** Transform the team into a high-performing platform engineering function that had a clear product identity, a compelling roadmap, and measurable impact on the organisation.

**Action:** I reoriented the team around four changes. Product identity: I renamed the team "Developer Experience Platform" and reframed their mission from "support DevOps tickets" to "make every engineer at this company faster and more confident." This was not cosmetic — it changed who the team talked to (developers, not just managers), what they built (self-service tooling, not bespoke pipelines), and how they measured success. Customer research: I introduced quarterly developer experience surveys and monthly office hours. The first survey revealed that the top three friction points were slow pipeline feedback (55 minutes), complex environment setup (average 3 days to onboard a new service), and lack of observability into deployment health. These became the team's first roadmap items. Team composition and growth: I hired a fourth engineer with a focus on developer tooling UX — someone who thought about CLI design and documentation as seriously as code. I also created a clear career ladder: Platform Engineer I/II/Senior, with explicit expectations at each level centred on scope of impact (squad → product area → organisation). This gave the two engineers who had been considering leaving a visible growth path. Measurement: I defined three success metrics — pipeline pass rate across the organisation (quality signal), median time to onboard a new service (adoption signal), and developer satisfaction score from the quarterly survey (impact signal). These were reported monthly to the CTO.

**Result:** Neither engineer left; both were promoted within 18 months. Developer satisfaction with CI/CD tooling improved from 2.3/5 to 4.1/5 over four quarters. Median new service onboarding time dropped from 3 days to 4 hours. The team's roadmap was adopted as the authoritative source for platform investment decisions, presented at the engineering all-hands each quarter.

## Seniority Shift
| Level | Expected Depth |
|-------|---------------|
| **Entry** | Uses the platform team's tooling; provides feedback via surveys |
| **Mid** | Contributes to the platform team's backlog with specific requests; participates in office hours |
| **Senior** | Partners with the platform team on complex integrations; contributes to shared tooling; acts as a squad-level platform champion |
| **Lead** | Owns the platform team's identity, roadmap, hiring, career development, and success metrics; shifts the team from reactive helpdesk to product-minded developer experience function |

## Key Terms
| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Developer Experience Platform** | A platform engineering team whose primary mission is improving the speed, confidence, and satisfaction of software engineers — measured through tooling adoption and developer surveys |
| **Product Mindset (Platform)** | The approach of treating engineering squads as customers, conducting user research, maintaining a prioritised roadmap, and measuring outcomes rather than outputs |
| **Developer Experience Survey** | A periodic survey measuring engineers' satisfaction with tooling, pipeline speed, and development workflow, used to drive platform roadmap prioritisation |
| **Platform Career Ladder** | A defined progression path for platform engineers that rewards increasing organisational scope of impact — from squad-level to product-area to organisation-wide influence |
| **Office Hours** | Scheduled, open-access sessions where engineers can interact with the platform team about tooling problems, feature requests, and integration questions |
| **Reactive Helpdesk** | The anti-pattern where a platform team spends its capacity on one-off requests and support tickets rather than building reusable infrastructure with multiplied impact |
| **Service Onboarding Time** | The elapsed time from a decision to create a new service to its first deployment through a fully configured CI/CD pipeline — used as a developer experience metric |
| **Platform Success Metrics** | The defined measures by which a platform team's impact is evaluated — pipeline health, adoption rates, and developer satisfaction — distinct from feature delivery metrics |
