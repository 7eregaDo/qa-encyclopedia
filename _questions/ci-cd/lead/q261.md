## The Intent
The interviewer is testing whether you can think about CI/CD quality at the organisational level — not just configuring pipelines, but defining where the whole organisation needs to go, how to get buy-in, and how to sequence the journey. This is a strategic, multi-year leadership challenge, not a technical implementation task.

## The STAR Script
**Situation:** I joined a company as Head of Platform Quality where 22 engineering teams had wildly inconsistent CI/CD practices. Some had mature pipelines with automated quality gates; others had no CI at all. Leadership wanted a single coherent direction but had no framework for assessing the current state or sequencing the investment.

**Task:** Assess the current CI/CD quality maturity across all teams, define a target state, and produce a roadmap that gave leadership a credible, funded plan for improvement.

**Action:** I developed a five-level maturity model specific to CI/CD quality, based on DORA research and the team's context. Level 1 — Manual: no automated pipeline, manual testing before release. Level 2 — Basic CI: pipeline runs tests on commit, but no quality gates, high flakiness. Level 3 — Gated CI: multi-stage pipeline with quality gates, low flakiness, environment parity. Level 4 — Continuous Delivery: automated promotion through environments, feature flags, canary deployments, DORA metrics tracked. Level 5 — Continuous Deployment: deploy on merge, automated rollback, synthetic monitoring, elite DORA performance. I assessed all 22 teams against this model in a two-week audit, using pipeline configuration, DORA data, and interviews. Results: 4 teams at Level 1, 9 at Level 2, 7 at Level 3, 2 at Level 4, 0 at Level 5. I set a 12-month target: 0 teams at Level 1, all teams at Level 3 minimum, 5 teams at Level 4, 2 teams at Level 5. I translated this into a sequenced investment plan: platform team builds the shared pipeline library and ephemeral environment tooling first, enabling the Level 2→3 transition for all teams simultaneously; then coaching for Level 3→4 transitions for the highest-traffic services; then Level 4→5 for the two most mature teams as reference implementations. I presented the roadmap, cost estimate, and expected DORA outcomes to the CTO and engineering VPs, securing a full-year platform team allocation.

**Result:** At the 12-month review: 0 teams at Level 1, 17 at Level 3+, 4 at Level 4, 1 at Level 5. Organisation-wide deployment frequency increased from 2.1 to 5.8 per week. Change failure rate dropped from 14% to 6%. The maturity model was subsequently adopted by the company's enterprise customers as a self-assessment tool.

## Seniority Shift
| Level | Expected Depth |
|-------|---------------|
| **Entry** | Works within a pipeline; may not be aware of cross-team CI/CD variation |
| **Mid** | Improves the pipeline for their team; benchmarks against other teams informally |
| **Senior** | Designs best-practice pipelines; identifies maturity gaps within their product area; drives improvements with data |
| **Lead** | Defines the org-wide maturity model; audits all teams; builds the investment roadmap; secures executive alignment; sets measurable DORA targets; creates reference implementations that others follow |

## Key Terms
| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **CI/CD Maturity Model** | A framework that defines progressive levels of pipeline capability, used to assess current state and sequence improvement investments |
| **Maturity Assessment** | A structured evaluation of each team's pipeline capability against the maturity model, producing a baseline and identifying gaps |
| **Reference Implementation** | A fully-realised example of the target state pipeline, built by the most mature team and used as a template for others to follow |
| **Platform Investment** | Dedicated engineering capacity for building shared CI/CD tooling that multiple teams consume, enabling level-up transitions without per-team bespoke work |
| **DORA Benchmark** | The comparison of an organisation's DORA metric values against the Elite/High/Medium/Low performer bands from the State of DevOps research |
| **Roadmap Sequencing** | The prioritisation of improvement investments based on impact per team, dependencies between levels, and available platform capacity |
| **Engineering Maturity** | The degree to which an organisation's engineering practices are systematic, measurable, and continuously improving — used as evidence of capability in executive and investor contexts |
| **Coaching Investment** | Dedicated senior QE or platform engineer time spent with individual teams to guide their level-up transition, distinct from platform tooling investment |
