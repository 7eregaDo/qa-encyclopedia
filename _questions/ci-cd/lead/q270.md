## The Intent
The interviewer is testing the highest-level analytical and communication skill: translating operational data into strategic insight. CI/CD data is rich with signal about engineering organisational health, team autonomy, and systemic bottlenecks — but it takes a lead-level QE to extract and present those insights to executives in a way that changes decisions. They want to see that you've done this and had impact.

## The STAR Script
**Situation:** The CTO was considering a significant organisational restructure — splitting two large multi-team product areas into smaller, more autonomous squads. There was significant debate about whether smaller squads would deliver faster or create coordination overhead. The decision was being made on intuition and anecdote because nobody had presented quantitative evidence.

**Task:** Use CI/CD and delivery data to provide an evidence base for the restructure decision.

**Action:** I pulled 12 months of pipeline data across all 18 teams — deployment frequency, lead time for changes, change failure rate, and pipeline duration. I also pulled team size data and mapped it against the metrics. The analysis showed a clear pattern: the four largest teams (8–12 engineers) had the lowest deployment frequency (under 1 per week) and the highest lead times (median 6.8 days). The six smallest teams (3–5 engineers) had deployment frequencies of 4–6 per week and lead times under 24 hours. Critically, the small teams' change failure rate was not higher — it was lower (4.2% vs 9.8%), suggesting that smaller team size was not trading quality for speed. I also analysed the pipeline data for the large teams and found they had the highest rate of pipeline conflicts and merge queue delays — a structural indicator of coordination overhead. I supplemented the CI/CD data with cycle time data from JIRA and interview data from a sample of engineers from large and small teams. I presented the analysis in a 15-minute slot at the quarterly engineering leadership offsite: a three-slide summary with the key charts, the key finding ("smaller team size correlates with 4x higher deployment frequency at equal quality"), and a recommendation to restructure with a suggested team topology. I was explicit about the limitations of the data — correlation, not causation — and presented the restructure as a hypothesis to validate, not a certainty.

**Result:** The CTO approved a restructure into smaller squads, explicitly citing the deployment frequency and change failure rate data as the deciding evidence. Six months post-restructure, the previously large teams' deployment frequency had improved from under 1 to 3.2 per week. The analytical approach — using CI/CD data to inform org design decisions — was adopted as a standard input to future restructure discussions.

## Seniority Shift
| Level | Expected Depth |
|-------|---------------|
| **Entry** | Collects pipeline data for their own use; not involved in strategic decision-making |
| **Mid** | Reports pipeline metrics to engineering management; identifies team-level patterns |
| **Senior** | Analyses DORA metrics across teams; identifies systemic patterns; presents data-driven recommendations to engineering leadership |
| **Lead** | Uses CI/CD data as an evidence base for org design and investment decisions at executive level; presents multi-dimensional analyses to CTOs and VPs; frames findings as hypotheses with explicit limitations; influences strategic decisions that outlast any individual pipeline configuration |

## Key Terms
| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Delivery Data Analysis** | The systematic examination of pipeline and DORA metrics across teams to identify patterns, bottlenecks, and correlations that inform engineering strategy |
| **Team Topology** | The structural organisation of engineering teams — their size, mission, and interaction patterns — which has documented effects on delivery frequency and lead time |
| **Merge Queue Delay** | The time a PR spends waiting to merge due to conflicts or queue length in a large team's shared codebase, a measurable indicator of coordination overhead |
| **Org Design Decision** | A strategic choice about how to structure teams, reporting lines, and responsibilities — which CI/CD data can inform by showing how current structures correlate with delivery outcomes |
| **Correlation vs Causation** | The statistical distinction between two variables moving together (correlation) and one causing the other (causation); important to acknowledge when presenting data-driven recommendations |
| **Cycle Time** | The elapsed time from a work item being started to its delivery to production, measured from project management data and used alongside pipeline metrics for full-stack delivery analysis |
| **Hypothesis-Driven Decision** | A decision framed as a testable hypothesis with defined success criteria, rather than a certainty — enabling a structured evaluation of the outcome after implementation |
| **Engineering Leadership Offsite** | A strategic meeting of engineering senior leaders where data-driven analyses and proposals are presented to inform major organisational and investment decisions |
