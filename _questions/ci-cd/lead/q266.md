## The Intent
The interviewer wants to know whether you operate as a strategic partner to product and business leadership, not just as a technical executor. CI/CD strategy that is disconnected from product delivery commitments creates invisible risks — teams commit to launch dates without knowing whether their pipeline can support the required deployment cadence. A lead should be in the room when those commitments are made.

## The STAR Script
**Situation:** I was Head of Platform Quality at a company that had just announced a major product launch in five months. The launch required 12 teams to coordinate releases across three interdependent services, deploy to three new geographic regions simultaneously, and support a 10x traffic spike at launch. Nobody had consulted the platform team about whether the current CI/CD infrastructure could support this.

**Task:** Assess the platform's readiness to support the launch commitments and surface any gaps early enough to address them.

**Action:** I conducted a launch readiness assessment against four dimensions. Deployment coordination: the launch required 12 teams to deploy specific versions simultaneously. The current pipeline had no cross-service deployment coordination — teams deployed independently. I designed a release train mechanism: a single pipeline that assembled the required versions from each service, ran a combined integration test, and deployed to all three regions in sequence. This was built and tested two months before the launch. Geographic expansion: the pipeline only knew about one region. I worked with the infrastructure team to extend the deployment jobs to all three regions with region-specific health checks and a progressive rollout (one region at a time with a 10-minute stability window). Traffic spike readiness: I added a load test to the pre-launch pipeline that validated each service could handle 10x normal load. Three services failed the load test in month two — giving the engineering teams two months to optimise. Rollback complexity: simultaneous deployment of 12 services across three regions had no rollback plan. I designed and tested a coordinated rollback procedure that could revert all 12 services in all regions to the last stable version within 15 minutes. I presented the readiness assessment and gap closure plan at the monthly product leadership meeting at month three — framing each gap as a delivery risk with a mitigation status.

**Result:** The launch deployed on schedule with zero incidents. The coordinated rollback was not needed but was tested successfully in a pre-launch drill. The load test findings led to three performance optimisations that also benefited day-to-day performance. The product VP subsequently requested platform readiness as a standard input to all major launch planning sessions.

## Seniority Shift
| Level | Expected Depth |
|-------|---------------|
| **Entry** | Executes the release checklist for launches; not involved in launch planning |
| **Mid** | Contributes pipeline readiness information to launch planning; flags known pipeline limitations to engineering management |
| **Senior** | Conducts pipeline readiness assessments for major launches; designs and tests launch-specific pipeline capabilities; presents risks to engineering leadership |
| **Lead** | Participates in product launch planning as a platform readiness partner; defines and executes comprehensive readiness assessments; designs cross-service coordination mechanisms; presents delivery risk to product and business leadership |

## Key Terms
| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Launch Readiness Assessment** | A structured evaluation of whether the CI/CD platform can support the deployment requirements of a planned product launch, identifying gaps with sufficient lead time to address them |
| **Release Train** | A pipeline that coordinates the simultaneous deployment of multiple services at specific versions, ensuring release consistency across interdependent systems |
| **Cross-Service Deployment Coordination** | The mechanism by which multiple services are deployed in a controlled sequence with shared integration validation, rather than independently |
| **Geographic Region Rollout** | A progressive deployment strategy that deploys to one region at a time with stability validation before proceeding to the next, reducing the blast radius of a launch failure |
| **Platform Readiness** | The assessed capability of the CI/CD platform to support a product initiative's deployment, scaling, and operational requirements |
| **Delivery Risk** | The probability and impact of a product commitment being delayed or degraded due to an unresolved platform capability gap |
| **Coordinated Rollback** | A rollback procedure that reverts multiple interdependent services simultaneously to a consistent prior state, preserving system integrity |
| **Pre-Launch Drill** | A planned, full-scale rehearsal of the launch deployment and rollback procedures in a production-like environment, verifying that the procedure works before it is needed |
