## The Intent
The interviewer is testing your incident response thinking and your ability to remain professional and structured under pressure. Discovering a critical vulnerability in production is one of the most stressful moments in a QE's career. They want to see clear-headed prioritisation, appropriate escalation, and no impulse to either dismiss the finding or publicly disclose it prematurely.

## The STAR Script
**Situation:** During a routine exploratory testing session on the production environment (read-only, with authorisation), I discovered that the `GET /admin/users` endpoint was accessible without any authentication — it returned the full user list including email addresses, hashed passwords, phone numbers, and account creation dates for all 85,000 registered users.

**Task:** Handle the discovery responsibly — assess, contain, escalate, remediate, and communicate — without causing additional harm.

**Action:** I followed a structured response. Immediate verification: I made one additional request to confirm the finding was genuine and not a test environment misconfiguration, then stopped testing — no further exploitation or data collection. Documentation: I documented the finding privately with the exact request, the response (a small excerpt with data redacted), the timestamp, and the environment confirmed as production. Immediate escalation: within 10 minutes I had a direct message to the security lead and engineering manager with the finding summary marked as critical. I did not raise it in a public Slack channel or defect tracker. I asked for a call to discuss containment. Containment: in the emergency call (15 minutes after discovery), the team agreed to disable the endpoint immediately via a feature flag while a proper authentication check was implemented. The fix deployed within 40 minutes of the initial discovery. Impact assessment: I helped the team assess whether the endpoint had been accessed by any unauthorised parties — reviewing access logs for non-authenticated requests to the endpoint in the previous 30 days. Two suspicious access patterns were identified and flagged for the data protection officer. Formal reporting: I wrote a concise incident report covering the timeline, the vulnerability (missing authentication on admin endpoint), the data exposed, the containment action, the access log findings, and the remediation plan.

**Result:** The endpoint was secured within 40 minutes. The DPO assessment concluded the two suspicious accesses warranted a precautionary notification to the affected users. The post-incident review identified that admin endpoints were not included in the pre-launch security checklist — this was corrected. No regulatory action was required, partly due to the speed of containment.

## Seniority Shift
| Level | Expected Depth |
|-------|---------------|
| **Entry** | Knows to stop testing, not exploit further, and escalate immediately to the security lead |
| **Mid** | Follows a structured response — verify, document, escalate privately, support containment, participate in impact assessment, write the incident report |
| **Senior** | Leads the incident response; coordinates with DPO, legal, and engineering; defines the post-incident process improvements; manages external disclosure if required |

## Key Terms
| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Responsible Disclosure** | The practice of reporting a security vulnerability to the affected organisation and allowing a reasonable remediation window before any public disclosure |
| **Containment** | The immediate action taken to reduce the attack surface of a confirmed vulnerability — such as disabling an endpoint or revoking a compromised credential — while a permanent fix is developed |
| **Impact Assessment** | The evaluation of a confirmed vulnerability's real-world impact, including whether it was exploited, what data was exposed, and the regulatory and reputational consequences |
| **Data Protection Officer (DPO)** | The person within an organisation responsible for overseeing data protection compliance and managing regulatory notification obligations under GDPR |
| **Access Log Review** | The analysis of server or application logs to determine whether a vulnerability was accessed by unauthorised parties prior to its discovery and containment |
| **Incident Report** | A formal document capturing the timeline, nature, impact, containment actions, and remediation plan for a security incident |
| **Feature Flag Containment** | The use of a feature flag to instantly disable a vulnerable endpoint or feature in production without requiring a code deployment or release cycle |
| **Precautionary Notification** | Communication sent to potentially affected users when a data exposure may have occurred, even without confirmed exploitation, as a good-faith protective measure |
