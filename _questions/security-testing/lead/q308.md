## The Intent
The interviewer is testing whether you can measure the effectiveness of a security programme with the same rigour that engineering leadership applies to delivery performance. Security is historically measured by the absence of incidents — which is not useful for driving investment or demonstrating progress. They want to see a metrics framework that distinguishes leading from lagging indicators and drives genuine programme improvement.

## The STAR Script
**Situation:** As Head of Application Security, I was reporting to the CISO quarterly but had no established security metrics. Reports were narrative — "we ran three pen tests, found 47 issues, fixed 38 of them" — with no trend data, no benchmark comparison, and no forward-looking indicators. The CISO wanted a metrics framework that would support board-level reporting and justify the programme's budget.

**Task:** Design a security KPI framework that distinguished programme health (leading indicators) from security outcomes (lagging indicators) and was credible at board level.

**Action:** I defined eight KPIs across three categories. Programme activity (leading indicators — what we're doing): Security coverage — percentage of services assessed at each tier (automated baseline, enhanced manual, pen tested) in the trailing 12 months. Target: 100% at baseline, 80% of high-risk services at enhanced. SAST/DAST pass rate — percentage of pipeline runs passing security gates without a High+ finding. Target: above 95%. Programme effectiveness (leading indicators — how well we're doing it): Mean time to remediate — average days from finding to confirmed fix, by severity. Target: Critical ≤7 days, High ≤30 days, Medium ≤90 days. Finding recurrence rate — percentage of findings in the current period that represent a recurring vulnerability class from a prior period. Target: below 10%. Security debt trend — the count of open findings by severity over time (is the backlog growing or shrinking?). Security outcomes (lagging indicators — what's happening): Production security incidents per quarter — incidents attributable to application security failures. Security-related customer impact — customer-hours of service disruption or data exposure caused by security incidents. Regulatory findings — findings from regulatory assessments (ICO, FCA) attributable to application security. I built a Grafana dashboard for operational use and a one-page executive summary for the CISO/board. I reviewed all eight KPIs monthly with my team and presented the summary quarterly to the CISO.

**Result:** The metrics framework was adopted by the CISO for board reporting within two quarters. The MTTR metric revealed that High-severity findings were averaging 47 days to remediate — significantly over the 30-day target — triggering a remediation priority review. Over 12 months, MTTR for High findings dropped from 47 to 22 days. The board specifically cited the security debt trend chart as the clearest evidence of programme improvement.

## Seniority Shift
| Level | Expected Depth |
|-------|---------------|
| **Entry** | Reports on individual findings; not involved in programme metrics |
| **Mid** | Tracks finding counts and resolution rates for their area |
| **Senior** | Defines KPIs for a product area's security programme; produces trend reports for engineering leadership |
| **Lead** | Designs the org-wide security KPI framework; distinguishes leading and lagging indicators; builds executive dashboards; uses metric trends to drive programme decisions; presents to CISO and board |

## Key Terms
| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Leading Indicator** | A metric that predicts future security outcomes — such as coverage or MTTR — allowing proactive management before incidents occur |
| **Lagging Indicator** | A metric that reflects past security outcomes — such as incident frequency — providing evidence of programme effectiveness but not enabling proactive intervention |
| **Mean Time to Remediate (MTTR)** | The average elapsed time from a security finding being confirmed to its fix being verified, used to measure remediation velocity by severity tier |
| **Finding Recurrence Rate** | The percentage of findings that represent a vulnerability class previously identified, indicating whether root causes are being addressed or just symptoms |
| **Security Debt** | The accumulation of open, unresolved security findings — tracked as a count and trend to show whether the programme is paying down risk or accumulating it |
| **Security Coverage** | The proportion of services that have received the defined depth of security testing for their risk tier within the measurement period |
| **SAST/DAST Pass Rate** | The percentage of pipeline runs completing security scanning stages without blocking High or Critical findings, measuring the security health of ongoing development |
| **Board-Level Security Dashboard** | A concise visual summary of the most important security KPIs, designed for non-technical executives to understand programme health and trend at a glance |
