## The Intent
The interviewer is assessing crisis leadership — whether you can remain structured and effective under maximum pressure, coordinate across engineering, legal, and executive functions, and emerge from an incident with both the immediate problem resolved and the organisation's security posture genuinely improved. This is one of the highest-stakes situations a security QE lead can face.

## The STAR Script
**Situation:** I was Director of Application Security Engineering when a security researcher contacted us through our vulnerability disclosure programme with evidence of a critical vulnerability: an unauthenticated endpoint was exposing the full account details of all 140,000 registered users. The researcher had proof-of-concept access but had not exploited it beyond confirming the vulnerability. The endpoint had been live for 11 days.

**Task:** Lead the incident response from initial triage through containment, investigation, notification, and post-incident improvement.

**Action:** I ran the response across five concurrent workstreams. Immediate containment (hour 1): I called the on-call engineering lead and we disabled the endpoint via feature flag within 22 minutes of the researcher's report. I confirmed the fix with the researcher and communicated our 90-day coordinated disclosure timeline. Technical investigation (hours 1–24): I led a forensic review of the access logs for the 11-day exposure window. We identified 847 requests to the endpoint from 12 distinct IP addresses beyond our own monitoring. Three IP addresses showed patterns consistent with automated enumeration. I documented the exposure window, affected data fields, and suspected access patterns. Legal and regulatory (day 1): I escalated to the DPO and General Counsel within 2 hours. Under GDPR Article 33, we had 72 hours to notify the ICO of a likely reportable breach. I prepared the ICO notification with the DPO — confirmed reportable, submitted within 68 hours. External communication (day 2): I worked with the communications team on the customer notification. I advocated for transparent disclosure — naming the vulnerability class, the exposure window, the data involved, and our remediation — rather than a vague "security incident" notice. Executive briefing (day 1 and weekly thereafter): I briefed the CEO and board with a structured summary: what happened, what data was exposed, what we did, what the regulatory outcome was likely to be, and what systemic changes would prevent recurrence. Post-incident (weeks 2–4): I ran a blameless post-mortem identifying three systemic failures — no authentication test for admin endpoints in the deployment checklist, no monitoring alert for unauthenticated access to sensitive data, and no automated endpoint inventory. All three became programme initiatives.

**Result:** The ICO investigation was closed with a reprimand (no fine), specifically citing our rapid containment and transparent notification as mitigating factors. The researcher was thanked publicly and awarded a bug bounty. The three post-incident programme initiatives were completed within six weeks. The board cited the incident response quality as evidence of programme maturity.

## Seniority Shift
| Level | Expected Depth |
|-------|---------------|
| **Entry** | Reports potential security incidents immediately; does not attempt to investigate independently |
| **Mid** | Participates in incident response; performs initial triage; documents findings accurately; follows the escalation procedure |
| **Senior** | Leads the technical investigation; coordinates containment; prepares technical findings for legal and executive audiences |
| **Lead** | Owns the end-to-end incident response across technical, legal, regulatory, and communications workstreams; briefs the board; manages regulatory notification; runs the post-incident programme; manages external researcher relationships |

## Key Terms
| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Vulnerability Disclosure Programme** | A formal channel through which external security researchers can report vulnerabilities to an organisation, with defined response commitments |
| **Coordinated Disclosure** | An agreement between a researcher and an organisation that the vulnerability will not be publicly disclosed until a fix is deployed, typically within a 90-day window |
| **GDPR Article 33** | The regulation requiring notification to the supervisory authority (ICO in the UK) within 72 hours of becoming aware of a personal data breach likely to result in risk |
| **ICO (Information Commissioner's Office)** | The UK data protection regulator responsible for enforcing GDPR and investigating reportable data breaches |
| **Blameless Post-Mortem** | A structured review of an incident that focuses on systemic causes and process improvements rather than individual fault, preserving psychological safety |
| **Feature Flag Containment** | Disabling a vulnerable endpoint or feature via a configuration change rather than a code deployment, enabling immediate containment without a release cycle |
| **Forensic Log Review** | A systematic analysis of access logs to determine what data was accessed, by whom, and over what time period following a security incident |
| **Bug Bounty** | A financial reward paid to a security researcher who responsibly discloses a vulnerability, incentivising responsible disclosure and recognising the researcher's contribution |
