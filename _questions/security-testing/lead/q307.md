## The Intent
The interviewer is testing your ability to manage external security specialists as a programme owner, not just commission ad-hoc tests. A well-managed pen test programme is more than "hire a firm, get a report." It requires scoping decisions, vendor management, finding prioritisation, remediation tracking, and using the outputs to improve internal practices. They want to see programme thinking.

## The STAR Script
**Situation:** As Director of Application Security, I inherited a pen testing approach where the company hired an external firm once a year, received a report of 50–80 findings, remediated the critical ones before the next audit, and repeated the cycle. The programme was expensive (£60,000 per engagement), findings were not tracked to closure, and the same classes of vulnerability recurred year-on-year because the underlying root causes were never addressed.

**Task:** Redesign the external penetration testing programme to be more targeted, more effective, and integrated with the internal security programme.

**Action:** I made five changes. Scope-focused engagements: rather than a single annual whole-application test, I moved to three targeted tests per year — one for the highest-risk new feature area (timed to the product roadmap), one for the payment and authentication flows (always high-risk), and one for any regulatory compliance scope (required for FCA/SOC 2 evidence). Each engagement was shorter (5 days vs 10) and more focused, producing more useful findings per pound spent. Vendor selection and management: I created a vendor scorecard — technical capability, methodology transparency, report quality, and willingness to provide re-test verification. I rotated vendors every two years to get different perspectives. For each engagement, I provided the vendor with our internal threat model and the findings from the previous test, so they could focus on areas we had not tested ourselves. Finding lifecycle governance: I created a finding tracker in Jira with SLAs — Critical: 7 days, High: 30 days, Medium: 90 days, Low: next annual review. The CISO reviewed overdue findings monthly. Re-test: I required the vendor to verify each Critical and High finding after remediation — not just take the engineering team's word for it. Root cause analysis: for any finding that had appeared in a previous test, I ran a root cause session to understand why it recurred — was it a systemic gap in our testing, a training gap, or a process failure? The root cause drove a programme improvement, not just a fix.

**Result:** Annual pen test spend reduced from £60,000 to £45,000 while testing coverage of the highest-risk areas increased. Year-on-year recurrence of the same finding class dropped from 40% to 8% (root cause analysis was addressing systemic issues). The SOC 2 auditor cited the programme's finding lifecycle governance as a model control.

## Seniority Shift
| Level | Expected Depth |
|-------|---------------|
| **Entry** | Participates in pen test remediation as directed |
| **Mid** | Tracks findings to closure; provides technical context to the vendor; runs re-tests for remediated findings |
| **Senior** | Scopes pen test engagements; manages vendor relationships; defines finding SLAs; produces the remediation evidence |
| **Lead** | Designs the full pen testing programme; makes vendor selection and rotation decisions; integrates pen test findings with internal programme; conducts root cause analysis to prevent recurrence; presents programme to CISO and auditors |

## Key Terms
| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Scope-Focused Engagement** | A penetration test with a defined, targeted scope — such as a specific feature area or regulatory perimeter — rather than a broad whole-application assessment |
| **Finding Lifecycle Governance** | A process that tracks security findings from discovery to confirmed remediation, with defined SLAs and escalation paths for overdue items |
| **Vendor Scorecard** | A structured evaluation of a penetration testing firm based on technical capability, methodology, report quality, and re-test provision |
| **Re-Test Verification** | A follow-up assessment by the original pen testing vendor confirming that remediated findings have been genuinely fixed, rather than accepting the engineering team's self-certification |
| **Root Cause Analysis (Security)** | The investigation of why a security vulnerability existed and was not caught internally, used to drive programme improvements that prevent the same class of issue recurring |
| **Recurrence Rate** | The percentage of penetration test findings that appeared in a previous engagement, used as a measure of programme improvement effectiveness |
| **Vendor Rotation** | The practice of engaging different penetration testing firms on a defined cycle to introduce fresh perspectives and reduce the risk of blind spots from a single vendor's approach |
| **Threat Model Handoff** | The provision of the organisation's internal threat model and previous test findings to the incoming pen testing vendor, enabling them to focus on genuinely untested areas |
