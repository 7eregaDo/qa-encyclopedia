## The Intent

The interviewer is assessing executive presence and strategic influence. Senior QA leaders must be able to operate in leadership conversations — not just provide data on request, but proactively use quality intelligence to shape decisions before they are made. They want to see that you understand power dynamics, stakeholder motivation, and the art of landing a data-driven argument effectively.

---

## The STAR Script

**Situation:** The CPO was planning to accelerate a major platform migration by cutting the migration testing phase from six weeks to two, citing a competitive deadline. The migration involved moving 40,000 customer records and replacing the core billing engine. I had serious concerns — the billing engine was the highest-risk component in the product, and two weeks was insufficient for the data integrity and regression coverage required.

**Task:** I needed to influence the CPO to either restore the testing timeline or scope down the migration to a level that could be tested safely in two weeks — without being dismissed as risk-averse or obstructive.

**Action:** I requested a 30-minute slot with the CPO and CTO together — not separately. I came with three things. First, **historical data with direct relevance**: I pulled the defect history for every previous billing-related change in the past 18 months. Billing changes had the highest defect escape rate of any system area — 41% of billing-related changes had produced at least one production defect, compared to a 12% average across other areas. Second, **a cost model**: using our average incident response time and the value of affected customer contracts, I estimated that a billing defect affecting even 5% of migrating customers could result in £180,000 in emergency support, SLA credits, and churn risk. Third, **a scoped alternative**: rather than just saying "two weeks isn't enough," I proposed migrating 20% of customers in week one (lowest-value, lowest-risk cohort), running full regression and data integrity checks, and only proceeding with the full migration if the first cohort was clean. This preserved the competitive deadline for the majority of customers while managing the risk of a catastrophic failure.

**Result:** The CPO accepted the phased approach. The first cohort migration revealed a data transformation error affecting subscription renewal dates — caught in data integrity testing before it touched high-value customers. The full migration completed three weeks later, cleanly. The CPO later said the phased proposal had been the key — it gave them a way to hit the deadline without accepting the full risk.

## Seniority Shift

| Level | Expected Depth |
|-------|---------------|
| **Entry** | Share a defect report or test result with a direct manager to inform a decision. |
| **Mid** | Build a data case to influence a product or engineering decision at team level. |
| **Senior** | Proactively bring quality intelligence to leadership decisions, frame risk in business terms, and offer constructive alternatives that allow the business to move forward safely. |

---

## Key Terms

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Quality Intelligence** | Proactive use of quality data to inform and shape decisions — before being asked. |
| **Executive Framing** | Presenting technical risk in business language — revenue, customer impact, competitive risk — for a leadership audience. |
| **Phased Migration** | Rolling out a major change incrementally, starting with a low-risk cohort before full deployment. |
| **Data Integrity Testing** | Verification that data is accurate, complete, and uncorrupted after a transformation or migration. |
| **Decision Timing** | Intervening in a decision while it is still open — before commitments are made that are difficult to reverse. |
