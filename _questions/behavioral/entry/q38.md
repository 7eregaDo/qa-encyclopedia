## The Intent

The interviewer is checking for self-awareness and professionalism. They want to know that you won't cut corners when bored, that you have genuine strategies for maintaining focus, and ideally — that you use repetitive work as a signal to automate. Saying "I just push through it" is honest but incomplete. Saying "I automate everything" is often unrealistic at entry level. The best answer balances honesty about the challenge with concrete coping strategies.

---

## The STAR Script

**Situation:** During a three-week regression cycle ahead of a major release, I was executing the same 80-test manual regression suite for the fifth time in two weeks. The tests were familiar, the steps were identical, and I was aware my attention was starting to drift.

**Task:** I needed to maintain the same level of rigour on test five as I had on test one — because that is exactly when a regression suite fails to catch something.

**Action:** I used three strategies. First, **vary the approach within the constraint**: instead of always executing tests in the same order, I shuffled the sequence on alternate runs. This kept my brain slightly more engaged and occasionally surfaced issues that sequential execution had missed (a later test would interact unexpectedly with state left by an earlier one). Second, **connect the work to its purpose**: when I noticed attention fading, I reminded myself specifically what each test area was protecting — the payment flow protects revenue, the login flow protects every single user. This sounds simple but it genuinely reset my focus. Third, **flag automation candidates**: every time I executed a test that felt purely mechanical — identical inputs, deterministic outputs, no judgment required — I added a comment in the test case noting it as an automation candidate. By the end of the cycle, I had a prioritised list of 23 tests that I proposed to automate in the next sprint, directly reducing the manual burden in future cycles.

**Result:** No coverage failures on that regression cycle despite the repetition. The automation proposal was approved and 18 of the 23 tests were automated within the following month, permanently reducing the manual regression time by 35%.

## Seniority Shift

| Level | Expected Depth |
|-------|---------------|
| **Entry** | Demonstrate genuine strategies for maintaining focus and quality during repetitive work, and show awareness of automation as the long-term solution. |
| **Mid** | Describe how you identified and prioritised automation opportunities from manual work, built the business case, and delivered the automation. |
| **Senior** | Discuss the strategy for eliminating repetitive manual work at scale — automation coverage targets, tool selection, CI integration, and rebalancing the team's time toward higher-value exploratory and risk-based testing. |

## Key Terms

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Regression Suite** | A set of test cases re-executed after each build to confirm existing functionality is unbroken. |
| **Attention Drift** | The gradual reduction in focus and rigour that occurs during repetitive tasks — a primary source of missed defects. |
| **Automation Candidate** | A test case that is sufficiently stable, deterministic, and repetitive to be a strong candidate for automation. |
| **Test Sequencing** | The order in which test cases are executed — varying it can reveal state-dependent defects. |
| **Intrinsic Motivation** | Staying engaged by connecting to the purpose and impact of the work, rather than relying on external reward. |
