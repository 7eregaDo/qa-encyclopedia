## The Intent

The interviewer wants to see that you don't freeze, thrash between tasks, or silently drop work when overloaded. They are looking for a structured approach to triage, proactive communication about trade-offs, and evidence that you can make deliberate decisions under time pressure rather than reactive ones.

---

## The STAR Script

**Situation:** On the final day of a sprint, I had three simultaneous demands: retesting five fixed defects so they could be closed before the sprint review, executing smoke tests on a hotfix that needed to go to production that afternoon, and writing test cases for two new stories that had been pulled forward from the next sprint.

**Task:** Everything felt urgent, but not everything was equally important. I needed to sequence my work deliberately and communicate clearly so nothing critical was dropped without the team knowing.

**Action:** I spent five minutes mapping each task against two dimensions: business impact if delayed and time sensitivity. The hotfix smoke test was the highest priority — a production deployment was waiting and blocking another team. Defect retests were second — closing them before the sprint review was visible to stakeholders and the sprint couldn't close cleanly without them. The new test cases were third — important but not blocking anything today. I communicated this sequence in the standup: "I'll do the hotfix smoke test first, then defect retests, then new test cases — I may not complete all of the new cases today, which means those stories may not be fully ready for tomorrow. Is that acceptable?" The product owner confirmed the sequence was correct and that test cases could carry over. I then worked through the list without switching tasks mid-stream.

**Result:** The hotfix was tested and deployed on time. All five defects were retested and closed before the sprint review. Three of the five new test cases were completed that day — the remaining two were finished the following morning with no impact on the sprint. The team appreciated that I'd flagged the trade-off proactively rather than silently dropping something.

## Seniority Shift

| Level | Expected Depth |
|-------|---------------|
| **Entry** | Show a clear prioritisation logic (impact + urgency), proactive communication about trade-offs, and focused execution. |
| **Mid** | Describe managing competing priorities across multiple stakeholders with conflicting interests, and negotiating scope transparently. |
| **Senior** | Discuss managing team-level workload prioritisation, capacity planning, and building systems that prevent chronic overload rather than just surviving it reactively. |

---

## Key Terms

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Prioritisation Matrix** | A framework for ranking tasks by urgency and importance to guide sequencing decisions. |
| **Time Sensitivity** | How quickly a task must be completed before it causes a downstream problem. |
| **Business Impact** | The consequence to users, stakeholders, or the organisation if the task is delayed or dropped. |
| **Proactive Communication** | Raising trade-offs and risks before they become problems, rather than after. |
| **Context Switching** | Moving between tasks before completing them — reduces focus and increases error rate. |
