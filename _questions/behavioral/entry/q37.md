## The Intent

The interviewer is assessing initiative, problem-solving, and the ability to influence team practice beyond your own work. They want to see that you observe your environment, identify genuine pain points, and act on them — rather than waiting to be told what to improve. Even small, well-executed improvements signal the right professional instincts.

---

## The STAR Script

**Situation:** Our team had a recurring problem during sprint handoffs: developers would mark stories as "ready to test" in Jira, but the test environment often wasn't set up, test data wasn't seeded, or the build hadn't been deployed yet. I would pick up a story, spend 20 minutes setting up context, and discover it wasn't actually testable. This happened multiple times per sprint.

**Task:** I wanted to eliminate the wasted setup time and the frustration on both sides — developers didn't realise how much time was being lost, and I didn't want to be raising the same complaint without a solution.

**Action:** I proposed a simple "ready to test" checklist — three items that had to be confirmed before a story moved to the testing column: (1) build deployed to test environment, (2) test data seeded or documented, (3) any dependent stories also deployed. I created a Jira ticket template with the checklist built in as a comment that developers could tick off before transitioning the status. I introduced it in a retrospective — not as a complaint, but as a time-saving proposal — and asked the team if they'd be willing to trial it for one sprint. One developer pushed back slightly, feeling it added overhead. I acknowledged the concern and offered to keep it lightweight — no approval needed, just a self-check before status change.

**Result:** The team adopted the checklist without further objection. In the following sprint, I had zero instances of picking up an untestable story. Developer feedback after two sprints was positive — they appreciated knowing exactly what was expected before handoff. The checklist was added to our team wiki as a permanent working agreement.

## Seniority Shift

| Level | Expected Depth |
|-------|---------------|
| **Entry** | Identify a specific pain point, propose a practical solution, handle mild resistance, and show a measurable result. |
| **Mid** | Describe a more complex process change affecting multiple team members or spanning the sprint lifecycle — with before/after metrics. |
| **Senior** | Lead an organisation-wide process improvement initiative — building a case, managing change resistance, piloting, measuring, and scaling adoption. |

---

## Key Terms

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Process Improvement** | An intentional change to how work is done, aimed at reducing friction, waste, or error. |
| **Working Agreement** | A set of shared team practices that everyone has agreed to follow. |
| **Retrospective** | The sprint ceremony where teams reflect on process and agree on improvements — the natural home for proposing changes. |
| **Definition of Ready** | Criteria a story must meet before it enters a sprint or testing phase. |
| **Adoption Friction** | The resistance or effort required for a team to start using a new practice — lower friction increases uptake. |
