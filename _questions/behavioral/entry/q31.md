## The Intent

The interviewer is assessing your judgment under pressure and your ability to communicate difficult news clearly. They want to see that you don't bury problems, panic, or simply defer the decision to someone else. They are also checking whether you understand that raising a critical defect late is not a failure — hiding it is.

---

## The STAR Script

**Situation:** Two days before a scheduled public launch of a new checkout flow, I discovered that entering a promo code during checkout caused the order total to calculate incorrectly — in some cases charging customers more than the displayed price.

**Task:** I needed to assess the severity, communicate the finding to the right people immediately, and support the team in making a release decision — all without triggering panic or causing a blame spiral.

**Action:** I documented the defect thoroughly within 20 minutes: steps to reproduce, affected scenarios, screenshots, and an impact statement in plain language ("customers may be overcharged at checkout in any transaction using a promo code"). I immediately flagged it to the lead developer and product owner together — not separately — so the conversation about impact and options happened once, with everyone present. I prepared a quick triage: which promo codes triggered the bug, what percentage of expected launch traffic would use them, and whether a temporary workaround (disabling promo codes at launch) was feasible. I stayed available for the next three hours to retest any fix attempts and provide clear pass/fail feedback.

**Result:** The team decided to delay the launch by 24 hours rather than release with the defect or disable a heavily marketed feature. The fix was delivered and verified that evening. The launch proceeded the following day without incident. The product owner later said the structured defect report and triage made the decision straightforward — the data was already there.

## Seniority Shift

| Level | Expected Depth |
|-------|---------------|
| **Entry** | Demonstrate clear communication, fast escalation, and calm under pressure. Show you raised the issue rather than minimised it. |
| **Mid** | Add impact quantification, stakeholder management across multiple parties, and a structured recommendation (release / delay / workaround). |
| **Senior** | Lead the incident response, facilitate the release decision with full risk data, and implement post-release monitoring to catch any missed edge cases. |

## Key Terms

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Critical Defect** | A defect that causes significant user impact, data loss, or financial harm — typically requiring immediate action. |
| **Escalation** | The process of raising a concern to a higher level of authority or visibility when it cannot be resolved at the current level. |
| **Impact Statement** | A plain-language description of what the defect means for users or the business — separate from the technical description. |
| **Release Decision** | A formal or informal agreement by stakeholders on whether a build is ready for production deployment. |
| **Workaround** | A temporary measure that reduces the impact of a defect without fixing the underlying cause. |
