## The Intent

The interviewer wants evidence that you can work with developers as a partner, not an adversary. At entry level, they are specifically checking that you can communicate technically well enough to be useful in a debugging conversation, and professionally well enough to maintain a positive relationship under the stress of a difficult problem.

---

## The STAR Script

**Situation:** A defect I had raised — intermittent session timeouts affecting users mid-checkout — was proving extremely difficult to reproduce. The developer had attempted to fix it twice, but my retest found the issue still occurring. The ticket had been open for two weeks and frustration was growing on both sides.

**Task:** I needed to break the reproduce-fix-retest cycle by collaborating directly with the developer rather than continuing to exchange information asynchronously through the ticket.

**Action:** I asked the developer if we could do a 30-minute pairing session — me sharing my screen and attempting reproduction while they watched and asked questions. I came prepared with everything I knew: the exact steps, browser version, network conditions, time-of-day pattern I had noticed (it seemed more frequent in the afternoon), and logs I had captured from the browser console. During the session, the developer noticed something I had not: the timeout was correlated with a specific API call that was taking over 8 seconds. They hadn't been able to see this from the ticket alone because I hadn't included API response times in my reports — I hadn't known they were relevant. Together we identified that the issue only occurred when a third-party payment verification service was slow to respond. I then created a specific reproduction test case that artificially throttled the API response, making the issue reliably reproducible on demand.

**Result:** With a reliable reproduction case, the fix was implemented and verified within 24 hours. The developer said it was the fastest resolution of a complex intermittent bug they had experienced. I learned to include API response time data in all future intermittent defect reports.

## Seniority Shift

| Level | Expected Depth |
|-------|---------------|
| **Entry** | Show you can collaborate directly with developers, contribute technical context, and adapt your approach when the ticket queue isn't working. |
| **Mid** | Describe a situation involving cross-team debugging — coordinating between frontend, backend, and infrastructure to isolate a complex system issue. |
| **Senior** | Discuss building collaborative practices at team level — pair testing, embedded QA in development squads, and shared ownership of defect resolution metrics. |

---

## Key Terms

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Pairing Session** | A real-time collaborative working session where two people work on the same problem simultaneously. |
| **Intermittent Defect** | A defect that does not occur consistently — harder to reproduce and diagnose than deterministic bugs. |
| **Reproduction Case** | A precise, repeatable set of steps and conditions that reliably triggers a defect. |
| **Asynchronous Communication** | Exchanging information via tickets, comments, or messages — slower and less precise than synchronous pairing for complex issues. |
| **Root Cause** | The underlying technical reason a defect occurs — distinct from the symptoms observed during testing. |
