## The Intent

The interviewer is assessing whether you have operated beyond the engineering organisation and influenced product thinking at a strategic level. This is a question about organisational scope — they want to see that quality intelligence in your hands became a product decision, not just an engineering one. Candidates who have only influenced engineering decisions are operating below Lead-level scope.

---

## The STAR Script

**Situation:** Our quality data — specifically the pattern of defects and user-reported issues — was telling a story that the product team had not seen, because they were looking at feature usage metrics and NPS scores rather than defect taxonomy. I had been tracking defect patterns for 12 months and had noticed something significant: 58% of all defects in the previous two quarters were concentrated in the platform's notification and alert system — an area the product team considered stable and low-priority.

**Task:** I wanted to bring this pattern to the CPO as a product strategy signal — not as a quality report, but as evidence that a product area the business was treating as mature was actually a significant source of user friction and engineering cost.

**Action:** I prepared a 10-minute briefing specifically for the CPO, framed entirely in product terms. I did not open with defect counts — I opened with user impact: "Our data suggests that one in five active users encounters a broken or missing notification in any given month. Here is what that means for the workflows they rely on notifications to trigger." I then showed the defect concentration data, the engineering time being consumed in reactive fixes (approximately 2.5 engineer-weeks per quarter), and — most compellingly — three verbatim customer support tickets where users had described the notification failures causing them to miss critical business events. I connected this to the product roadmap: the notification system was scheduled for a minor refresh in Q3, but the data suggested it needed a structural investment, not a refresh. I proposed that the CPO consider repositioning the notification work from a minor Q3 item to a Q2 strategic priority, backed by a business case: fixing the system properly would reduce engineering maintenance cost by an estimated 60% and address a friction point affecting 20% of the user base monthly.

**Result:** The CPO pulled the notification work forward to Q2 and elevated it from a minor refresh to a full redesign with dedicated resource. The redesigned notification system launched in Q2, and user-reported notification issues fell by 74% in the following quarter. The CPO told me in a subsequent leadership meeting that the briefing had been the most useful quality-to-product connection they had seen — because it spoke the language of user experience and business cost, not testing metrics. I was subsequently invited to present quarterly quality insights directly to the product leadership team — a standing engagement that had not existed before.

## Seniority Shift

| Level | Expected Depth |
|-------|---------------|
| **Entry** | Share a defect observation with a product owner that leads to a requirement change. |
| **Mid** | Use defect pattern data to influence a product decision at sprint or quarter level. |
| **Senior** | Present quality intelligence to product leadership as strategic input, influencing roadmap prioritisation. |
| **Lead** | Build a standing relationship with product leadership as a quality intelligence partner — routinely influencing product strategy through quality signals translated into user and business impact language. |

---

## Key Terms

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Quality Intelligence** | The use of quality data — defect patterns, user reports, test signals — as strategic product and business insight. |
| **Defect Taxonomy** | A classification of defects by area, type, and impact — enables pattern recognition that raw defect counts do not. |
| **Product Strategy Input** | Quality insight presented in product terms — user impact, friction, and business cost — rather than engineering or testing terms. |
| **Strategic Repositioning** | Elevating a product area from low-priority to strategic investment based on evidence of its impact. |
| **Standing Engagement** | A recurring forum where quality intelligence is presented to product leadership — builds influence through consistent contribution. |
| **User Friction** | The points in a user's experience where the product creates difficulty, confusion, or failure — often visible in defect patterns before NPS or usage data reflects it. |
