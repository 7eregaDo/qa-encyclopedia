## The Intent

The interviewer is assessing whether you can operate at the level of organisational strategy — not just programme delivery. Lead-level QA professionals are expected to set direction, not just execute within it. They want to see that you can articulate a compelling quality vision, translate it into a roadmap, and sustain alignment around it across multiple teams and leadership layers over an extended time horizon.

---

## The STAR Script

**Situation:** I was appointed VP of Quality at a scale-up that had grown from 30 to 180 engineers in 18 months through a combination of organic growth and two acquisitions. Quality practices were fragmented — three different testing philosophies, four different toolchains, and no shared definition of what "good" looked like. The CTO asked me to present a three-year quality vision at the next engineering leadership offsite.

**Task:** I needed to produce a vision that was credible to engineers, compelling to the business, and actionable enough to survive the offsite and become a real roadmap — not a slide deck that was forgotten by Monday.

**Action:** I built the vision through three stages. First, **discovery with breadth**: over four weeks I conducted 30-minute interviews with 22 people — engineers, QA leads, product managers, customer success, and the CFO. I was not looking for solutions; I was building a shared picture of the current reality and the pain it caused. The CFO told me that quality-related customer churn was costing an estimated £1.2M annually. That number became the North Star for the vision — not a testing metric, but a business outcome. Second, **vision definition**: I structured the vision around three horizons. Horizon 1 (year 1): unified foundation — common toolchain, shared quality metrics, baseline automation coverage across all teams. Horizon 2 (year 2): embedded quality — QA integrated into every squad, shift-left practices standard, contract testing across all service boundaries. Horizon 3 (year 3): predictive quality — AI-assisted test generation, production quality monitoring as a first-class engineering signal, quality as a competitive differentiator cited in sales conversations. Each horizon had three to four measurable outcomes, not activities. Third, **communication strategy**: I presented the vision at the offsite not as a QA strategy document but as a business strategy — opening with the £1.2M churn figure and the question "what would our growth trajectory look like if that number was zero?" I then walked through the three horizons as business outcomes, not engineering practices. After the offsite, I published a one-page vision summary in the engineering wiki, held a town hall for all QA engineers, and established a quarterly quality leadership review where horizon progress was reported against measurable outcomes.

**Result:** The vision received full executive sponsorship — the CTO referenced it in the company all-hands two weeks later. Year 1 outcomes were achieved within 13 months. By the end of year 2, quality-related churn had fallen by 68% — £816,000 in recovered revenue. The vision became the basis for a dedicated quality engineering budget line for the first time in the company's history.

## Seniority Shift

| Level | Expected Depth |
|-------|---------------|
| **Entry** | Describe following a quality strategy set by others and contributing to its goals. |
| **Mid** | Define a quality approach for a single team or product area with measurable targets. |
| **Senior** | Build a QA strategy for a product organisation with phased implementation and clear metrics. |
| **Lead** | Define a multi-year quality vision at organisational level — rooted in business outcomes, communicated across all layers, and sustained through a structured governance model. |

---

## Key Terms

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Quality Vision** | A strategic narrative describing where the organisation is going with quality and why it matters at a business level. |
| **Horizon Planning** | Structuring a multi-year strategy into phases with distinct goals — typically stabilise, build, and optimise. |
| **North Star Metric** | A single, business-level outcome metric that anchors the vision and measures ultimate success. |
| **Executive Sponsorship** | Active, visible support from C-level or VP-level leadership — essential for sustaining a multi-year programme. |
| **Quality Governance** | The structures, forums, and rituals that keep a quality programme visible and accountable over time. |
| **Predictive Quality** | A mature quality model that uses production signals, AI, and historical data to anticipate defects before they occur. |
