## The Intent

The interviewer is assessing organisational design capability and people leadership under change. Lead-level QA professionals must be able to adapt their function's shape and purpose in response to business context — not defend the status quo. They want to see that you can hold the human and structural dimensions of a restructure simultaneously, and that you communicate and execute change without destroying trust or losing key talent.

---

## The STAR Script

**Situation:** The company made a strategic decision to move from a B2C product model to a B2B enterprise model. This had profound implications for QA — B2B enterprise customers required formal test evidence, compliance documentation, and security testing capabilities that our existing team of five generalist manual and automation QA engineers was not structured to provide. At the same time, the B2C regression suite that had been the team's primary focus was being deprioritised as the product pivoted. I had to restructure the team without losing the people or the institutional knowledge they carried.

**Task:** I needed to redesign the team's structure, skill mix, and operating model to serve an enterprise customer base — within a quarter, without replacing the existing team.

**Action:** I worked through four dimensions. First, **skills assessment**: I ran individual conversations with each of the five engineers to understand their interests, adjacent skills, and development aspirations — not just their current capabilities. Two were interested in security testing, one had prior experience with compliance documentation from a previous role, and two wanted to deepen their automation expertise. The restructure could be built around the people I had. Second, **role redefinition**: I redesigned the team around three capability areas rather than the previous generalist model: security and compliance testing (two engineers, with a training investment in OWASP and ISO 27001 basics), enterprise integration and API testing (two engineers focused on the complex integration patterns B2B customers required), and automation infrastructure (one engineer owning the CI/CD quality pipeline). I gave each capability area an informal lead — no title change, but clear ownership and a development path. Third, **transparent communication**: I held a team meeting where I explained the business shift in full — what the move to B2B meant for customers, for the company, and for QA specifically. I presented the restructure as an opportunity for the team to grow into a higher-profile, more strategically visible function, not as a threat. I was honest about what would be harder and what would be more interesting. Fourth, **supported transition**: I created a 90-day transition plan for each engineer with specific learning goals, pairing arrangements, and check-in milestones. No one was thrown into a new role without a ramp.

**Result:** All five engineers remained through the restructure — zero attrition. Within six months, the team was producing enterprise-grade test evidence packages that became a standard deliverable in the sales process. The security testing capability caught two compliance-relevant vulnerabilities before the first enterprise customer audit. The VP of Sales cited the QA documentation capability as a differentiator in two enterprise deals. The restructure became a case study the CTO referenced when discussing how engineering functions should adapt to business model changes.

## Key Terms

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Organisational Design** | The deliberate structuring of a team's roles, capabilities, and reporting relationships to achieve a strategic goal. |
| **Skill Mix** | The combination of capabilities across a team — intentionally designed to match the work the team needs to do. |
| **Change Management** | The structured approach to transitioning people from a current state to a new one with minimal disruption and attrition. |
| **Capability Area** | A cluster of related skills and responsibilities within a team — a less rigid alternative to rigid job titles. |
| **Retention Through Change** | Keeping key people during a restructure by providing transparency, development opportunity, and human respect. |
| **Enterprise QA** | Quality assurance practices designed for enterprise customers — including compliance documentation, formal test evidence, security testing, and SLA verification. |
