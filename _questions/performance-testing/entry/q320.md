## The Intent
The interviewer is checking whether you can communicate across technical boundaries. Performance data in raw form is meaningless to a product manager or business owner. A QA engineer who can translate numbers into decisions is far more valuable than one who dumps a JMeter report into Slack and calls it done.

## The STAR Script
**Situation:** After running a pre-launch load test for a new booking flow, I had a full JMeter report: percentile tables, throughput graphs, error logs. The product owner needed to decide whether to proceed with the release.

**Task:** I needed to produce a summary that gave the product owner a clear answer — go or no-go — with enough context to understand the basis for the recommendation.

**Action:** I created a one-page summary with three sections. First, a traffic summary: "We tested 150 simultaneous users — equivalent to peak load based on last month's analytics." Second, a results table with five metrics, each marked pass (green) or fail (red) against the agreed SLA. Third, a plain-language finding: "The checkout page passed all targets. The payment confirmation step exceeded the 3-second threshold at high load — this affects approximately 8% of users at peak. Recommend proceeding with a fix scheduled for the next sprint." I attached the full JMeter report as an appendix for the engineering team.

**Result:** The product owner made an informed go decision with a documented risk. The summary format was adopted as the standard post-test deliverable.

## Seniority Shift
| Level | Expected Depth |
|-------|---------------|
| **Entry** | Can produce a clear pass/fail summary with plain-language explanations, targeted at a non-technical reader |
| **Mid** | Tailors the report to the audience — executive summary, engineering detail, and business risk framing — and owns the go/no-go recommendation |
| **Senior** | Builds automated reporting into the pipeline (e.g., Grafana dashboards, Slack alerts) and translates results into cost, user impact, and revenue risk terms for leadership |

## Key Terms
| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Pass/fail threshold** | A pre-agreed performance target that determines whether a metric is acceptable (e.g., average response time must be under 2 seconds) |
| **Go/no-go decision** | The release decision informed by performance test results — whether the system is ready to go live |
| **Executive summary** | A concise high-level section of a report that states the conclusion and key findings without technical detail |
| **SLA (Service Level Agreement)** | The defined performance commitments — internal or contractual — that test results are validated against |
| **User impact statement** | A plain-language description of how a performance issue affects the end user experience (e.g., "1 in 12 users will wait more than 5 seconds at checkout during peak traffic") |
| **Risk-based recommendation** | A test conclusion that quantifies the severity and likelihood of the issue and recommends a proportionate response rather than a binary pass/fail |
| **Appendix** | The section of a test report containing raw data, full metric tables, and tool output — referenced by the summary but not required reading for non-technical stakeholders |
