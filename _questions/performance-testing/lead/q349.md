## The Intent
The interviewer is checking whether you can contextualise the performance discipline within the broader values and responsibilities of a technology organisation. At lead level, performance is not purely a technical metric — it is a lens through which equity, inclusion, and environmental responsibility can be understood and acted upon.

## The STAR Script
**Situation:** Our organisation had published commitments to digital accessibility and a net-zero carbon target for technology operations. The sustainability team was working on data centre energy reduction, and the accessibility team was focused on screen reader compatibility — but neither had engaged with performance engineering, despite the direct connections.

**Task:** I identified the opportunity to connect performance engineering to both programmes and proposed a joint initiative.

**Action:** I prepared a brief for both teams outlining the connection. For accessibility: I pulled analytics data showing that 23% of our users were on devices over four years old and 18% connected on mobile data with below 10 Mbps throughput. Our Lighthouse CI data showed that three key journeys had a Time to Interactive exceeding 8 seconds on a simulated 4G connection — making them functionally unusable for those users. I framed performance optimisation as an accessibility enabler: reducing JavaScript bundle size and implementing lazy loading improved the experience for constrained users without any additional accessibility-specific engineering. For sustainability: I worked with our cloud infrastructure team to correlate compute utilisation data with our AWS carbon footprint tool. I found that optimising two database queries in the reporting module (identified through performance testing) reduced average CPU utilisation by 14%, which the infrastructure team calculated as equivalent to approximately 4.2 tonnes of CO₂ per year across our fleet — a concrete environmental benefit from a performance fix. I presented a joint proposal: the performance engineering team would add environmental impact estimates to all bottleneck findings, and accessibility review would include performance profiling on constrained device profiles.

**Result:** The sustainability team included performance efficiency as a metric in the annual technology carbon report. The accessibility programme adopted Lighthouse CI constrained-device profiles as a standard check. Two engineers from other teams cited the framing as the reason they prioritised performance work that had previously seemed low-value — the broader meaning shifted their motivation.

## Seniority Shift
| Level | Expected Depth |
|-------|---------------|
| **Entry** | Understands that slow products affect users on constrained devices and connections more severely |
| **Mid** | Includes constrained-device profiles in front-end performance testing and can articulate the equity dimension |
| **Senior** | Designs test suites that explicitly cover constrained user contexts and advocates for performance as an accessibility concern |
| **Lead** | Connects performance engineering to organisational sustainability and accessibility commitments — building cross-disciplinary partnerships that broaden the impact and meaning of the performance discipline |

## Key Terms
| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Digital accessibility** | The practice of ensuring that digital products are usable by people with a wide range of abilities, devices, and connectivity conditions |
| **Constrained device profile** | A test configuration simulating a low-powered device and slow network connection (e.g., mid-range mobile on 4G) to represent users at the performance-disadvantaged end of the user population |
| **Time to Interactive (TTI)** | A performance metric measuring how long until a page is fully interactive — particularly important for users on slow connections or low-powered devices |
| **Carbon footprint tooling** | Cloud provider tools (e.g., AWS Customer Carbon Footprint Tool) that estimate the CO₂ emissions associated with compute workloads |
| **Compute efficiency** | The ratio of useful work performed to energy consumed — improved by optimising algorithms, reducing unnecessary computation, and eliminating waste |
| **Equity framing** | Presenting a technical quality concern (slow performance) as a fairness issue — certain user groups are systematically more affected by poor performance than others |
| **Cross-disciplinary partnership** | A formal or informal collaboration between two organisational functions (e.g., performance engineering and sustainability) that produces outcomes neither could achieve independently |
