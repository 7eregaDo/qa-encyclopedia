## The Intent
The interviewer is checking whether you can operate effectively in conditions of high ambiguity, inherited technical debt, and competing organisational priorities. At lead level, major structural events like M&A are a career-defining test of whether your capability generalises beyond the familiar context.

## The STAR Script
**Situation:** Our company acquired a smaller SaaS business. I was asked to lead the performance engineering workstream for the technical integration — combining two customer bases onto a single platform within nine months. The acquired company had no performance testing practice and we had no visibility into how their system behaved under load.

**Task:** I needed to assess the performance posture of the acquired system, identify the highest-risk integration points, and establish a performance readiness gate for the migration cutover.

**Action:** I structured the work in three phases. Phase 1 — discovery (weeks 1–4): I ran a series of exploratory load tests against the acquired system's staging environment, covering their top five traffic journeys. I identified two critical findings: their authentication service had no connection pooling and degraded severely above 80 concurrent users; their database had grown to 340 GB with no partitioning strategy, producing table scans on every report query. I documented both as integration blockers. Phase 2 — integration risk modelling (weeks 5–8): I modelled the combined load scenario — our 1,200 active users plus their 400 active users arriving on a shared platform — and identified that the combined authentication load would exceed the degradation threshold without remediation. I worked with the acquired team's engineering lead (now part of our organisation) to redesign the connection pooling configuration. Phase 3 — migration readiness gate (weeks 9–36): I defined a performance readiness checklist for cutover: all combined-load tests must pass at 1.5× projected peak, authentication p95 under 800 ms at 200 concurrent users, no memory growth trend over a 4-hour soak. The cutover was gated on this checklist — non-negotiable.

**Result:** The connection pooling fix was delivered in week 6. The combined load tests passed in week 28. The cutover proceeded on schedule and handled the combined user base without incident. The acquired team's engineers — who had never run a formal load test before — shipped the fix and ran the validation tests themselves, having been coached through the process. That capability transfer was as valuable as the technical remediation.

## Seniority Shift
| Level | Expected Depth |
|-------|---------------|
| **Entry** | Understands that integrating systems creates new performance risks that did not exist in either system independently |
| **Mid** | Can assess the performance posture of an unfamiliar system and identify specific risks |
| **Senior** | Designs the integration performance test programme and defines the cutover readiness gate |
| **Lead** | Leads the full performance engineering workstream for a major integration — discovery, risk modelling, remediation, readiness gate, and capability transfer to the acquired team |

## Key Terms
| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Performance posture** | The overall state of a system's performance health — including test coverage, baseline data, known issues, and maturity of practice |
| **Integration risk** | A performance risk that arises specifically from combining two previously separate systems — such as combined load on a shared component or data migration volume |
| **Exploratory load test** | A test run against an unfamiliar system to characterise its behaviour under load, without pre-defined pass/fail criteria — used during discovery phases |
| **Connection pooling** | A technique for maintaining a set of reusable database connections, avoiding the overhead of opening a new connection per request |
| **Migration cutover** | The event at which users are switched from the old system to the new one — a high-risk moment requiring performance readiness validation |
| **Readiness gate** | A formal checklist of performance criteria that must be met before a significant event (cutover, launch) proceeds |
| **Capability transfer** | The process of building performance testing knowledge and practice within an acquired or less mature team — a multiplier that sustains quality beyond the initial engagement |
