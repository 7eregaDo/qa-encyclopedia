## The Intent
The interviewer is checking whether you can bridge the gap between engineering data and executive decision-making. At lead level, your ability to influence budget, headcount, and architectural investment depends on your ability to make performance legible to people who do not think in milliseconds.

## The STAR Script
**Situation:** I had been leading the performance engineering function for eight months. We had strong internal data — SLO compliance rates, incident trends, test coverage — but the VP of Engineering struggled to explain performance health to the CPO and CEO in quarterly business reviews. Requests for infrastructure investment were being deprioritised because the business case was not landing.

**Task:** I was asked to prepare a one-page performance health summary for the quarterly executive review that would enable investment decisions without requiring the executives to understand performance testing methodology.

**Action:** I built the summary around three business-facing frames. Frame 1 — customer experience: "83% of customer-facing journeys met our speed SLO last quarter, up from 71% the previous quarter. The 17% that did not are concentrated in the reporting module — affecting primarily enterprise customers, who represent 34% of ARR." Frame 2 — revenue risk: I worked with the data team to pull the correlation between our checkout response time and conversion rate from our A/B test history. "Each 500 ms of checkout latency is associated with a 4.2% reduction in completed transactions, based on our own data. The checkout service is currently at 1.8 s p95 — within SLA, but 600 ms from the threshold. At current growth rates, we will breach the SLA in approximately 11 weeks without infrastructure investment." Frame 3 — investment options: Three options with cost and expected impact, framed as risk mitigation: do nothing (projected SLA breach in 11 weeks), incremental (read replica, £2,400/month, extends headroom by 6 months), architectural (async queue for reporting, 6 weeks engineering effort, permanent resolution). I excluded all tool names, metric names, and test methodology.

**Result:** The CPO approved the incremental option in the same meeting — the 11-week timeline made the risk concrete. The architectural option was added to the following quarter's roadmap. The one-pager format was adopted as the standard for all engineering health reporting at the quarterly review.

## Seniority Shift
| Level | Expected Depth |
|-------|---------------|
| **Entry** | Can explain performance results clearly to a non-technical colleague |
| **Mid** | Produces stakeholder-appropriate reports connecting metrics to user experience |
| **Senior** | Frames performance findings in terms of product risk and SLA compliance for engineering leadership |
| **Lead** | Translates performance health into revenue risk, customer trust, and investment options for C-level audiences — influencing budget and roadmap decisions |

## Key Terms
| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Business impact framing** | Expressing a technical metric in terms of a business outcome — revenue, customer retention, risk — rather than a performance value alone |
| **ARR (Annual Recurring Revenue)** | A business metric representing the annualised value of recurring subscription or contract revenue — used to quantify the customer impact of a performance issue |
| **SLO compliance rate** | The percentage of time or transactions during which a system met its defined Service Level Objective — a portfolio-level performance health metric |
| **Executive summary** | The top-level section of a report written for senior decision-makers, containing only the conclusion, business impact, and recommended options |
| **Risk timeline** | A projection of when a current trend (e.g., growing response time) will breach a defined threshold — converting a gradual problem into an urgent decision |
| **Investment options** | A structured presentation of two or three alternative responses to a performance risk, each with cost, effort, and expected impact — enabling an informed choice rather than a binary approve/deny |
| **Conversion rate correlation** | A data-driven relationship between a performance metric (response time) and a business outcome (completed purchases) — the most persuasive evidence for performance investment |
