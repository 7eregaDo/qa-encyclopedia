## The Intent
The interviewer is checking whether you can lead under pressure, across organisational boundaries, with incomplete information. At lead level, a performance incident is not a test execution problem — it is a coordination, communication, and decision-making challenge.

## The STAR Script
**Situation:** On a Black Friday morning, our e-commerce platform experienced a severe performance degradation: checkout response times exceeded 30 seconds for 40% of users. Revenue was being lost in real time. I was the most senior engineer on call. Three product teams and the infrastructure team were all involved, and no single team had the full picture.

**Task:** I took incident command and needed to coordinate investigation, maintain stakeholder communication, and drive toward a resolution decision under significant executive pressure.

**Action:** I ran the incident across four parallel tracks simultaneously. Track 1 — triage: I assigned each team a specific investigation scope with a five-minute reporting deadline. Database team: check query times and lock contention. Infrastructure: check CPU, memory, and auto-scaling status. Application team: check error logs and thread pool metrics. CDN team: check cache hit rates. Track 2 — communication: I set a 10-minute stakeholder update cadence via Slack — one message per update, in a dedicated incident channel, using a fixed format: current status, what we know, what we are doing, next update at. I kept the CPO updated with a parallel plain-language summary — no jargon, just business impact and direction. Track 3 — decision authority: I pre-agreed with the engineering VP that I had authority to make infrastructure changes without approval during the incident, to avoid approval delays. I used this to authorise scaling the database read replicas from 2 to 6 while investigation continued. Track 4 — resolution and recovery: the root cause emerged at T+22 minutes — a promotional campaign had triggered a marketing email to 800,000 users simultaneously, producing a traffic spike 4× higher than any we had modelled. The combination of cold cache (post-deployment) and spike had overwhelmed the connection pool. After scaling replicas and pre-warming the cache, response times normalised at T+41 minutes. I led the post-incident review two days later: five findings, three process changes, two architectural recommendations — all assigned and tracked.

**Result:** The platform recovered within 41 minutes. The post-incident review produced a marketing-engineering coordination process (campaigns above 100,000 recipients now require a 48-hour performance review), a cache pre-warming procedure for post-deployment periods, and a revised load model that included coordinated campaign traffic. No comparable incident has recurred in the three years since.

## Seniority Shift
| Level | Expected Depth |
|-------|---------------|
| **Entry** | Can participate in an incident investigation — collecting data, running queries, reporting findings |
| **Mid** | Leads the technical investigation for a performance incident within their domain and communicates findings clearly |
| **Senior** | Coordinates investigation across multiple system layers and drives toward root cause identification |
| **Lead** | Commands the full incident response — parallel investigation tracks, stakeholder communication at all levels, real-time decision authority, and post-incident systemic improvement |

## Key Terms
| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Incident command** | A structured role where one person takes overall coordination responsibility during an incident, preventing the chaos of multiple people giving conflicting directions |
| **Parallel investigation tracks** | Simultaneous, scoped investigation workstreams assigned to different teams — maximising information gathering speed under time pressure |
| **Stakeholder update cadence** | A pre-set frequency for incident status updates — reducing ad-hoc interruptions while keeping stakeholders informed |
| **Decision authority** | Pre-agreed permission to make specific classes of change (e.g., infrastructure scaling) without requiring approval during an incident — eliminating decision latency |
| **Post-incident review (PIR)** | A structured, blameless analysis of an incident conducted after resolution, producing findings and assigned remediation actions |
| **Blameless post-mortem** | A PIR conducted without attributing fault to individuals, focusing instead on systemic factors that enabled the incident |
| **Marketing-engineering coordination** | A cross-functional process ensuring that large-scale marketing actions (campaign sends, promotions) are reviewed for performance impact before execution |
