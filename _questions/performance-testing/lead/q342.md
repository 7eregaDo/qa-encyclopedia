## The Intent
The interviewer is checking whether you can think structurally about team design, not just technical delivery. At lead level, your leverage comes through the people you build around you — and the decisions you make about how the team operates shape what is achievable at scale.

## The STAR Script
**Situation:** Following the performance vision work I had led, the VP of Engineering approved headcount for a dedicated performance engineering function — three engineers to start, with a mandate to support all twelve product teams. I had never built a team from scratch and needed to make foundational decisions quickly.

**Task:** I needed to define the team's operating model, hire the first three engineers, establish ways of working, and demonstrate value within the first quarter.

**Action:** I made three foundational decisions before hiring. First, operating model: I chose a consultancy model over embedding — a small central team could not staff all twelve teams, so we would work on a request basis, set standards, and build capability rather than own delivery. Each product team retained responsibility for their own performance; we provided expertise, tooling, and coaching. Second, hiring profile: I wrote job descriptions that prioritised systems thinking and communication over tool-specific experience. I specifically looked for engineers who could explain a queuing model to a product manager and write a k6 script with equal comfort. I rejected profiles that were purely "JMeter operator" — expertise in one tool is a day-one skill; analytical reasoning is a career skill. Third, charter: I wrote a one-page team charter stating our purpose ("make performance a solved problem across the organisation"), our commitments (respond to requests within two working days, produce a written finding for every engagement), and our non-remit (we do not own product SLAs — product teams do). I published the charter to all engineering leads on day one of the team's existence, to set expectations before any relationship problems developed.

**Result:** The first quarter delivered four engagements: one capacity planning exercise, two pre-release load tests, and one incident post-mortem with recommendations. Each produced a written report shared back to the wider engineering organisation. By month six, inbound requests exceeded capacity — a problem I would rather have than the alternative. I used the demand data to make the case for a fourth hire.

## Seniority Shift
| Level | Expected Depth |
|-------|---------------|
| **Entry** | Understands the difference between embedded and centralised QA models |
| **Mid** | Can articulate the trade-offs of team structure models and contribute to hiring decisions |
| **Senior** | Shapes the operating model for their immediate team and manages senior individual contributors |
| **Lead** | Designs the team from inception — operating model, hiring philosophy, charter, growth path — and manages the organisational relationships required to sustain it |

## Key Terms
| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Consultancy model** | A centralised team operating model where specialists provide expertise on request to other teams rather than being permanently embedded |
| **Embedded model** | A team structure where specialists sit within product teams — higher context, lower scale |
| **Team charter** | A brief public document stating a team's purpose, commitments, and scope boundaries — managing expectations before misunderstandings occur |
| **Hiring philosophy** | A documented set of principles guiding what attributes and skills are prioritised in hiring, ensuring consistency and clarity across interviewers |
| **Non-remit** | An explicit statement of what a team does not own — as important as the remit for avoiding scope creep and unclear accountability |
| **Demand tracking** | Monitoring inbound requests and engagement volume to build an evidence base for headcount or resourcing decisions |
| **Growth path** | A defined progression of responsibilities and skills for team members — essential for retention in specialist disciplines where external market options are strong |
