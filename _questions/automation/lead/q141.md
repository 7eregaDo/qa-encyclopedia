## The Intent

The interviewer is assessing whether you can operate at the intersection of engineering strategy and organisational leadership. Lead-level automation professionals are expected to own the automation direction for the whole organisation — not just a team or product. They want to see structured thinking about investment priorities, coverage models, governance mechanisms, and how the strategy adapts to changing business context.

---

## The STAR Script

**Situation:** I was VP of Quality Engineering at a Series C company with 200 engineers across 15 product squads. Automation investment had been ad-hoc — some squads had mature suites, others had almost nothing. The board had asked the CTO for a three-year quality engineering roadmap, and I was responsible for the automation component.

**Task:** I needed to define a three-year automation strategy that was credible to engineers, compelling to the board, and governable at scale — with clear investment priorities, measurable outcomes, and an accountability model.

**Action:** I structured the strategy across four dimensions. First, **investment prioritisation by business risk**: I mapped the organisation's revenue exposure by product area and cross-referenced it with current automation coverage. Areas with high revenue exposure and low coverage were the highest-priority investment targets — not because they were the most technically interesting but because they carried the most business risk. This produced a risk-weighted coverage roadmap: Year 1 focused on the three highest-revenue product areas, Year 2 extended to the next tier, Year 3 embedded automation in the developer workflow organisation-wide. Second, **coverage targets by layer and risk tier**: I defined automation coverage targets as a portfolio — not a single percentage. Tier 1 (revenue-critical paths): 90% automated regression coverage, CI gate on every PR. Tier 2 (significant features): 70% coverage, CI gate on merge to main. Tier 3 (supporting features): 50% coverage, nightly run. These were targets with timelines, not immediate requirements — achievable within the three-year horizon. Third, **infrastructure investment**: I defined the shared infrastructure required to support the coverage targets — a central test results platform, a shared automation library, cloud browser farm access, and performance testing tooling. I costed each component and included it in the investment ask. Fourth, **governance model**: I established a quarterly automation review — attended by QA leads from each squad, the CTO, and myself — where coverage progress, flake rates, and investment utilisation were reviewed against the roadmap. Squad QA leads owned their coverage targets; I owned the organisation-wide outcome and the shared infrastructure.

**Result:** The three-year strategy was approved by the board with a £1.2M automation infrastructure budget over the period. At the end of Year 1, Tier 1 coverage had reached 84% across the three targeted product areas — ahead of the 80% Year 1 target. The quarterly review had caught two squads falling behind their coverage targets early enough to provide additional resource before they became a risk. The strategy document became the reference point for every automation hiring decision and tooling purchase request in the organisation.

## Seniority Shift

| Level | Expected Depth |
|-------|---------------|
| **Entry** | Follow the team's automation strategy and contribute tests toward defined coverage targets. |
| **Mid** | Define an automation strategy for a team — tooling, coverage targets, CI integration, and quarterly review. |
| **Senior** | Design a multi-team automation strategy with tiered coverage targets, shared infrastructure, and a governance model. |
| **Lead** | Define a three-year organisational automation strategy — business-risk-driven investment priorities, tiered coverage targets, infrastructure roadmap, board-ready cost justification, and a quarterly governance model with named accountability. |

---

## Key Terms

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Risk-Weighted Coverage Roadmap** | An automation investment plan prioritising coverage by business risk exposure rather than technical preference. |
| **Coverage Target Portfolio** | A set of differentiated coverage targets per risk tier — more rigorous for higher-risk areas, proportionate for lower-risk ones. |
| **Automation Infrastructure** | The shared platforms, libraries, and tooling that enable automation across multiple teams — distinct from individual team test suites. |
| **Quarterly Automation Review** | A governance forum reviewing coverage progress, suite health metrics, and investment utilisation against the strategy roadmap. |
| **Named Accountability** | Explicit ownership of outcomes — squad QA leads own team targets, the Lead owns the organisation-wide outcome. |
| **Three-Year Horizon** | The time frame for a long-term automation strategy — long enough to achieve structural change, short enough to remain relevant. |
