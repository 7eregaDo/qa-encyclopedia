## The Intent

The interviewer is assessing financial acumen and board-level communication. Lead-level automation professionals are expected to quantify and present the return on their organisation's automation investment with the same rigour a CFO would apply to any capital investment. They want to see a measurement methodology grounded in real data, conservative assumptions with documented rationale, and a presentation format appropriate for a non-technical board.

---

## The STAR Script

**Situation:** The board asked for an annual review of the £600,000 automation infrastructure investment approved two years earlier. The CTO asked me to prepare the ROI presentation — 15 minutes at the board meeting, with a one-page summary for the board pack. The board included two non-technical members who would be asking questions.

**Task:** I needed to present a credible, financially rigorous ROI assessment that was understandable to non-technical board members and defensible to the CFO who had been sceptical of the original investment.

**Action:** I built the ROI presentation around three measurement dimensions, each with a before/after comparison and conservative assumptions clearly documented. First, **cost avoidance from reduced manual testing**: before the investment, manual regression consumed an average of 9.2 FTE-weeks per release cycle across the organisation. Post-investment, with 68% automated coverage, this had dropped to 3.4 FTE-weeks. The saving was 5.8 FTE-weeks per cycle × 24 release cycles per year × £2,000/week (fully-loaded engineer cost) = £278,400 per year. I used the fully-loaded cost figure recommended by the CFO in the original business case — no inconsistency. Second, **defect cost reduction from earlier detection**: before the investment, 22 production incidents per year attributable to insufficient test coverage, averaging £14,000 each = £308,000. Post-investment, 11 production incidents of the same class = £154,000. Saving: £154,000 per year. I was explicit that I was attributing only incidents in areas where automation coverage had materially improved — not claiming credit for all incident reduction. Third, **velocity gain from faster release cycles**: the reduction in manual regression from 3 days to 4 hours per cycle had enabled the organisation to move from biweekly to weekly releases. The product team estimated that accelerated feature delivery had contributed to three enterprise contract wins totalling £420,000 in ARR. I presented this as an attributed contribution rather than a direct automation saving — clearly labelled as an estimate. Total quantified annual return: £432,400 (cost avoidance + defect reduction). Total investment (Year 1 + Year 2): £600,000. ROI at 24 months: 44% net return on investment, with ongoing annual return of £432,400 against a Year 3 maintenance cost of £120,000.

**Result:** The board approved a Year 3 maintenance budget and an additional £180,000 for expanding automation coverage to two new product areas acquired through a recent acquisition. The CFO noted that the conservative methodology — not claiming credit for effects they couldn't directly attribute — had made the numbers more credible than most investment reviews they had seen. One non-technical board member asked if the velocity gain figure could be higher — I explained the conservative attribution methodology, which they accepted.

## Seniority Shift

| Level | Expected Depth |
|-------|---------------|
| **Entry** | Track the time saved on a specific testing task after automation is introduced. |
| **Mid** | Calculate the break-even point and annual return for a team-level automation investment. |
| **Senior** | Present an automation ROI case to VP-level leadership using cost avoidance and defect reduction metrics. |
| **Lead** | Design and present a board-level automation ROI assessment — three-dimension measurement methodology, conservative assumptions with documented rationale, before/after comparison, and 24-month net return calculation — in a format appropriate for non-technical board members. |

## Key Terms

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Automation ROI** | The financial return on automation investment — quantified across cost avoidance, defect reduction, and velocity gain. |
| **Cost Avoidance** | The cost of manual testing effort that automation has eliminated — the most directly measurable ROI dimension. |
| **Defect Cost Reduction** | The reduction in production incident costs attributable to automation-enabled earlier defect detection. |
| **Velocity Gain** | The business value generated by faster delivery cycles enabled by automation — the most strategic but least directly attributable ROI dimension. |
| **Conservative Attribution** | Claiming ROI credit only for outcomes directly attributable to the investment — builds credibility with financially rigorous audiences. |
| **Net Return on Investment** | Total return minus total investment cost — the headline metric for a board ROI presentation. |
